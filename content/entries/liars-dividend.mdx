  The social profit earned when fake media becomes plausible enough that real evidence can be waved away as synthetic inconvenience.
plainDefinition: |
  The liar's dividend is the benefit people gain when the existence of convincing AI-generated or manipulated media lets them deny authentic evidence as fake.
whyExists: |
  AI has lowered the cost of producing persuasive synthetic audio, images, and video. That does not only create fake evidence. It also weakens confidence in real evidence by giving bad actors a ready-made excuse.
misuse: |
  The phrase can be used too theatrically, as though trust has already collapsed beyond recovery. Verification, provenance, and forensic methods still matter. The problem is not total epistemic ruin. It is a higher and more expensive burden of proof.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means journalists, courts, platforms, and the public need stronger provenance checks while opportunists claim every inconvenient recording is probably AI. The immediate damage is often delay, confusion, and public doubt rather than perfect deception.

The liar's dividend is one of the more serious AI terms because it describes a failure mode with no product demo. It arrives as a shrug, a doubt, and a very convenient excuse.
