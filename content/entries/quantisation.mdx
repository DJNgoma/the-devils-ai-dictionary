  Quantisation reduces the numeric precision of model weights and/or activations (for example to 8-bit or 4-bit formats) to cut memory and often increase throughput, with some accuracy trade-offs.

  Large models strain memory bandwidth and capacity. Lower precision is one of the main practical compression levers for serving.

  Benchmark your tasks after quantisation, watch outliers and long-context behaviour, and pick formats your hardware actually accelerates.
