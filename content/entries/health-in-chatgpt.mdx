plainDefinition: |
  Health in ChatGPT is an OpenAI experience that lets eligible users in the United States connect supported medical records and Apple Health data so ChatGPT can answer questions using that personal health context. It is an information and preparation tool, not a clinician or a diagnosis.
whyExists: |
  Health information is fragmented across portals, documents, measurements, and appointments. Bringing authorised records into a conversational interface can help people organise histories, understand terminology, prepare questions, and notice what information is missing.
misuse: |
  Connecting records is easily mistaken for granting clinical authority. The model does not become a doctor because it can retrieve a laboratory result, and availability to eligible US users does not make the feature universal. Sensitive context also raises privacy, consent, retention, and account-security questions that a polished summary cannot answer for the user.
practicalMeaning: |
  Check eligibility and supported sources, review what is connected, use the output to prepare rather than self-certify treatment decisions, and take urgent or consequential questions to a qualified professional. Organisations should not infer that a consumer feature satisfies their clinical, regulatory, or records-governance obligations.
