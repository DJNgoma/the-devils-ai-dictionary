plainDefinition: |
  A closed-weight model is one whose trained parameters are not publicly released, with access usually limited to an API, a hosted product, or selected partnerships.
whyExists: |
  Companies keep weights closed to control distribution, monetise access, manage abuse, protect training advantages, and avoid turning tomorrow's margin into today's torrent.
misuse: |
  The term gets stretched when "closed weight" is treated as a synonym for frontier quality, safety, or inevitability. It names an access model, not a guarantee of technical superiority or responsible deployment.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means the vendor controls the pricing, rate limits, updates, logging surface, and deployment geography. If the model improves, degrades, disappears, or becomes more expensive, your roadmap receives the news second-hand.
