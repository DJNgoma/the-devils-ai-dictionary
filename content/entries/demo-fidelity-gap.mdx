devilDefinition: |
  The distance between what the polished demo appears to promise and what the production workflow can survive without stage lighting.
plainDefinition: |
  Demo fidelity gap is the difference between the controlled conditions of a polished AI demonstration and the messier, slower, more failure-prone workflow the demo implicitly claims to represent.
whyExists: |
  Demos need curation. Production needs stamina. The term exists because people keep confusing the first for evidence of the second, especially when the prototype is elegant, the narration is confident, and the operational details have been kindly removed from view.
misuse: |
  Not every gap is fraud. Some are ordinary prototype limitations. The phrase becomes useful when the demo quietly omits the real cost, staffing, latency, failure handling, or data quality burden required for the promised workflow to exist outside the stage.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means hand-picked inputs, reset state, hidden human intervention, forgiving latency, omitted edge cases, and a later production plan that discovers governance, tooling, and bad customer behaviour all have speaking parts.
