devilDefinition: |
  The condition in which every spreadsheet becomes consciousness, every autocomplete becomes destiny, and every awkward sentence from a chatbot is treated as a sign of either godhood or revolt.
plainDefinition: |
  "AI Psychosis" is an internet-age critical phrase, not a formal scientific diagnosis. It describes distorted thinking around AI systems: compulsive over-belief, over-attribution of agency, or a habit of interpreting ordinary model behaviour as profound evidence of mind, prophecy, or civilisation-scale inevitability.
whyExists: |
  People need a label for the strange social effect AI systems can have on users, founders, pundits, and investors: obsession, magical thinking, and the loss of proportion. The term exists because "you are taking autocomplete far too personally" was too long for social media.
misuse: |
  It gets abused as a cheap insult for anyone enthusiastic about AI, which makes it less useful. Genuine interest in the field is not the same thing as delusional over-interpretation. The phrase is most credible when it points to exaggerated belief formation, not mere excitement or scepticism.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means someone has stopped evaluating systems by evidence and started treating outputs, roadmaps, or rumours as proof of exceptional intelligence, moral authority, or historical inevitability. It can show up as compulsive prompting, anthropomorphism, policy overreaction, or investment logic built on vibes.

The phrase is useful only if it stays disciplined. It should point to distorted interpretation around AI, not become a lazy way to pathologise disagreement.

If a conversation needs clinical language, use clinical language. If it needs cultural criticism, use this term with that narrower ambition.
