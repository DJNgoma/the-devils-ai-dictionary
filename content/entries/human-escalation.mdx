devilDefinition: |
  The moment the workflow admits that a person is no longer a decorative safeguard but the next system dependency.
plainDefinition: |
  Human escalation is the explicit handoff from an automated or agentic workflow to a person when risk, ambiguity, policy, or failure conditions exceed what the system is allowed to handle alone.
whyExists: |
  Not every problem should stay inside the loop. Some cases need judgement, authority, empathy, or accountability that the automated path does not possess. The term exists so teams can talk about the handoff itself rather than vaguely gesturing at human oversight.
misuse: |
  It gets collapsed into pious "human in the loop" language without asking who the human is, what context they receive, or whether they can actually resolve the case. An escalation path that dumps confusion onto a queue is still confusion, only with payroll.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means thresholds, queues, summaries, owner assignment, response targets, and the awkward realisation that a good escalation path needs product design, staffing, and operational discipline rather than merely a button labelled "send to human".
example: |
  "The escalation was technically successful. A confused customer, an under-briefed reviewer, and a missing audit trail had all been promoted to the same inbox."

Human escalation is valuable precisely because it is not automatic triumph. It is the point where the system confesses what it cannot safely finish on its own.
