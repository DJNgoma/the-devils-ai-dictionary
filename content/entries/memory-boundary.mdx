  A memory boundary defines what an AI system may remember, retrieve, update, forget, or expose across sessions, users, tools, and contexts.
whyExists: |
  Persistent memory can make systems more useful, but it also creates privacy, relevance, contamination, and access-control problems. Boundaries keep memory from becoming an unreviewed second database.
misuse: |
  It gets softened into a settings label. The real question is not whether the product "has memory", but what crosses between contexts and who can inspect or delete it.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means retention rules, user controls, retrieval scopes, redaction, tenant separation, auditability, and clear defaults for what should not be remembered at all.
