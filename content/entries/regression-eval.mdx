  A regression eval is an evaluation designed to catch previously fixed failures, degraded behaviours, or safety issues that reappear after model, prompt, data, or system changes.

  AI systems change through model updates, prompt edits, retrieval changes, tool revisions, and policy tuning. Regression evals help ensure old problems do not quietly return.

  They get treated as comprehensive quality tests. A regression eval usually protects against known failures; it does not prove the system is generally safe or good.

  In practice it means maintaining failure cases, running them before release, tracking pass rates over time, and refusing to delete uncomfortable tests because the dashboard looks nicer without them.
