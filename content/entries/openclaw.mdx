plainDefinition: |
  OpenClaw is a self-hosted personal AI assistant and gateway platform designed to do things, not just chat. Its own docs describe it as an assistant you run on your own devices, replying through surfaces such as Telegram, WhatsApp, Discord, iMessage, Slack, and WebChat while orchestrating tools, memory, and workflows through an always-on gateway.
whyExists: |
  Because the market got bored of systems that only generate text and started demanding systems that can actually execute tasks.
misuse: |
  By treating any autonomous workflow as magic, or by forgetting that giving an AI broad permissions turns "helpful assistant" into "possible incident". The interesting part is not that it can act. The interesting part is how narrowly, visibly, and safely it acts.
practicalMeaning: |
  A software layer that connects models to tools, interfaces, and actions so that the system can perform useful work across apps and services. In practice it is closer to an orchestration-and-gateway stack than a free-floating digital butler.

Systems like OpenClaw matter because they make the agent discussion less abstract. Once the assistant lives in Telegram, runs on your own box, and can touch real tools, the architecture stops being philosophical and becomes operational.
