  The provider's quiet way of reminding your agent that enthusiasm is not capacity planning.
plainDefinition: |
  A rate limit is a cap on how much an API or service can be used over a time period, often measured in requests, input tokens, output tokens, concurrent jobs, or other throughput units.
whyExists: |
  It exists to protect shared infrastructure, control abuse, preserve service quality, and make sure one ambitious integration does not turn a provider's capacity plan into a public experiment.
misuse: |
  It gets confused with spend limits, context limits, or general product restrictions. Those may interact, but they are not the same thing: one controls throughput, another controls money, and another controls how much the model can read at once.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means backoff logic, queues, retries, request shaping, model tier choices, budget alerts, and the discovery that your "real-time" agent is now waiting politely behind its own success.
example: |
  "The demo scaled beautifully until the rate limit arrived and taught the agent the ancient discipline of waiting."
