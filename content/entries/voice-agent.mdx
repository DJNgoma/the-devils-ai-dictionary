devilDefinition: |
  A synthetic colleague hired to answer the phone with perfect stamina and the occasional inability to notice that a human is furious.
plainDefinition: |
  A voice agent is an AI system that can conduct spoken conversations, usually by combining speech recognition, language models, and text-to-speech in order to handle calls, bookings, support, or outbound interactions.
whyExists: |
  It exists because phone-based workflows are expensive, repetitive, unevenly staffed, and still surprisingly central to sales, service, healthcare, logistics, and small business operations.
misuse: |
  It is oversold as if sounding natural were the same as being operationally competent. A smooth voice can still misunderstand intent, mishandle escalation, or create a synthetic version of the same bad call centre experience.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means handling routine conversations, qualification, scheduling, and after-hours coverage, with humans reserved for edge cases, regulated contexts, and the moments when empathy turns out to require more than latency optimisation.
