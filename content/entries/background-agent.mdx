plainDefinition: |
  A background agent is an agentic system designed to continue multi-step work outside a live chat session—often writing code, filing tickets, or producing artefacts that surface later via notification, PR, or inbox.
whyExists: |
  Useful work often takes longer than a single conversation window. Products needed a shape for long-running delegated tasks without blocking a human at the keyboard.
misuse: |
  Marketed as 'set and forget' without budgets, kill switches, audit logs, or clear ownership of side effects. Background does not mean unsupervised forever.
practicalMeaning: |
  Require explicit scopes, resource limits, progress traces, and human review points for irreversible actions. Treat background agents like batch jobs with credentials, not magic interns.

  "The background agent opened twelve PRs overnight and one production incident."
