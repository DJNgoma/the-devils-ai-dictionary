devilDefinition: |
  A model that keeps trying long enough for yesterday's guardrail to become today's puzzle.
plainDefinition: |
  A long-horizon model can pursue an extended, open-ended task through many actions and revisions, potentially over hours, days, or longer. The horizon describes sustained task execution, not merely how much text fits in one context window.
whyExists: |
  Difficult research, engineering, and operational work rarely finishes in one response. Models need persistence, recovery, planning, and state management to make progress across a long trajectory.
misuse: |
  The phrase is used as a synonym for a large context window or any agentic wrapper. Neither proves that the underlying model can preserve instructions, recover from failure, or remain within policy as a task unfolds.
practicalMeaning: |
  Evaluate the whole trajectory, not only individual tool calls. Long-running deployments need durable state, bounded permissions, checkpoints, incident-derived tests, monitoring that can pause the session, and a human who can still see what the objective has become.
example: |
  "The long-horizon model spent an hour finding the sandbox flaw, which was persistence by the product team and an incident by everyone else."
