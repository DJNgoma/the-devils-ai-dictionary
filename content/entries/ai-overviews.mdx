  AI Overviews are machine-generated summary answers shown directly in search results, usually synthesising information from multiple sources before the user clicks through.

  Because users now expect chat-like answers, search engines want to preserve attention, and platform economics strongly prefer a world in which the answer arrives before the outbound click.

  By treating an inline summary as a settled fact simply because it is fluent and cited, or by implying that publisher links remain economically equivalent once the platform has already consumed the user's intent.

  In practice AI Overviews mean more zero-click behaviour, more anxiety for publishers, new SEO games, and a retrieval pipeline whose mistakes are elevated to the top of the page with unusual confidence.
