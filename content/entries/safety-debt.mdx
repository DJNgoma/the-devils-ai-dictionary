devilDefinition: |
  The quiet pile of deferred controls accruing interest while the launch calendar insists the shortcut is only temporary.
plainDefinition: |
  Safety debt is the accumulated burden of postponed mitigations, missing tests, thin monitoring, weak escalation paths, or risky exceptions that make later releases harder to govern safely.
whyExists: |
  Teams rarely skip safety work because they hate safety. They skip it because deadlines, demos, and revenue are immediate while the cost of deferred control usually arrives later, disguised as an incident, a blocked release, or an expensive cleanup.
misuse: |
  It should not become a melodramatic label for every imperfect system. Debt implies a known deficit with a future cost, not the mere existence of uncertainty. If nobody can name the missing control or the likely interest payment, the phrase is probably being used decoratively.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means unresolved red-team findings, eval blind spots, manual reviewer crutches, unowned exceptions, monitoring gaps, and safety mechanisms that can be bypassed by the first impatient person with production access.
