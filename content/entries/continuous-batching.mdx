  Continuous batching is an inference-server scheduling technique that dynamically adds and removes requests from a running batch as prompts arrive and generations finish, keeping accelerators busier than static batching allows.

  Requests do not arrive in neat simultaneous groups, and generations end at different times. Static batches waste capacity waiting for stragglers or empty seats.
