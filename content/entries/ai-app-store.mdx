plainDefinition: |
  An AI app store is a marketplace or catalogue for agents, GPTs, tools, skills, MCP servers, templates, or other AI add-ons that users can discover, install, buy, or enable inside a platform.
whyExists: |
  It exists because platforms want ecosystems, developers want distribution, and users want reusable capabilities without building every integration themselves. The app-store shape is familiar; the trust model is less comforting.
misuse: |
  It gets abused when listing becomes credibility. An AI app, agent, or skill may carry instructions, tool access, data permissions, hidden maintenance risk, and a cheerful description written by the party most excited about adoption.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means review processes, permissions, versioning, security scanning, developer identity, revenue share, uninstall paths, data access disclosures, and a policy for removing agents that are popular for the wrong reasons.
