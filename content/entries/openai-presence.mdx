  An enterprise agent platform named after the quality most carefully simulated by a support line.
plainDefinition: |
  OpenAI Presence is a managed platform for deploying voice and chat agents into defined enterprise workflows. It combines model reasoning with scoped system access, policies, guardrails, simulations, evaluations, escalation rules, and a Codex-assisted process for proposing improvements.
whyExists: |
  A production support or internal-service agent needs more than a model endpoint. Organisations must define its job, connect approved systems, test edge cases, monitor live sessions, transfer difficult cases to people, and update behaviour as products and policies change.
misuse: |
  The name is mistaken for telepresence, a general agent runtime, or a self-serve builder. Presence is a limited-general-availability product delivered to eligible enterprise customers with OpenAI engineers and selected integrators; its managed process does not make every workflow suitable for automation.
practicalMeaning: |
  Start with one bounded job, grant only the knowledge and actions it needs, define approval and escalation rules, simulate failures, and compare proposed revisions with the production version before rollout. The customer still owns the policy choices and consequences.
example: |
  "Presence resolved the billing query, escalated the exception, and ensured that both actions appeared in the same triumphant case study."
