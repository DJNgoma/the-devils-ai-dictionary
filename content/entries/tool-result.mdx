plainDefinition: |
  A tool result is the data, error, status, or structured payload returned by an external tool call in an AI workflow.
whyExists: |
  Agents and assistants need to distinguish between what an external system actually returned and what the model later says about it. That boundary matters for debugging, audits, citations, retries, and blame.
misuse: |
  It gets treated as though the model's summary and the tool's output are the same thing. They are not. A neat paragraph can misread a correct payload, hide a warning, or politely bury a failed call.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means preserving raw outputs, errors, timestamps, identifiers, and schemas long enough for the system to explain what happened after the model has finished being fluent.
example: |
  "The tool result said the account was inactive; the assistant's summary said the user was probably ready to upgrade, which was generous and entirely unhelpful."
