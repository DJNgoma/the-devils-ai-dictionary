  The long, expensive ritual in which a machine studies vast piles of data until the bill becomes spiritual.
plainDefinition: |
  Training is the process of adjusting a model's weights using data and optimisation algorithms so that the model performs better on a task or distribution of tasks. In large models, this includes repeated forward and backward passes over massive datasets.
whyExists: |
  The term exists because models do not arrive preloaded with competence. Their behaviour is shaped by exposure to data, objective functions, optimisation choices, and huge amounts of compute.
misuse: |
  It gets used as if any change to a model counts as training. Not quite. Prompting is not training. Calling an API is not training. Even many "learning" product features are really retrieval, memory, or rule changes layered around a frozen model.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means data pipelines, filtering, labelling, loss curves, GPUs, checkpoints, failed runs, parameter updates, evaluation cycles, and weeks or months of engineering effort. It is one of the least glamorous ways to spend a lot of money.

One of the most useful distinctions in AI is simple: training changes the model; inference uses it. Many confused product conversations improve immediately once that sentence is said aloud.
