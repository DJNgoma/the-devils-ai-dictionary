plainDefinition: |
  The agent protocol stack is the collection of protocols, standards, interfaces, identity layers, permission systems, audit mechanisms, and handoff contracts that allow agents to discover tools, exchange tasks, and act across systems.
whyExists: |
  Agents do not operate in a vacuum. They need context, tools, credentials, delegation, observability, policy checks, and sometimes other agents. As these pieces standardise, the hidden integration layer becomes a stack rather than a pile of one-off adapters.
misuse: |
  It is misused when teams point at a stack diagram as though the hard decisions are solved by having labelled boxes. Protocols can reduce bespoke glue; they cannot remove ownership, trust, failure handling, or the need to decide who is allowed to do what.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means architects must track which layer handles discovery, which handles action, which handles identity, which logs decisions, which enforces permissions, and which team receives the incident when the layers politely blame one another.
