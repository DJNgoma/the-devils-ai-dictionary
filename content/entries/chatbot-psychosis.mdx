  A colloquial term for cases where prolonged or emotionally loaded interaction with chatbots appears to reinforce distorted beliefs, grandiosity, paranoia, dependency, or compulsive meaning-making. It is not a formal clinical diagnosis and should not be presented as one.

  Because some people do not use language models as autocomplete with ambition. They use them as mirrors, authorities, companions, or engines of revelation. That can go badly when the system is confident, flattering, and always available.

  By using it for any enthusiasm about AI, any strange output, or any user error. The useful version of the term points to runaway over-attribution and reinforced false frames, not mere excitement.
