  A detector for hostile instructions, useful until someone mistakes a smoke alarm for the fire brigade.
plainDefinition: |
  A prompt injection scanner is a tool or model-based check that looks for malicious, conflicting, or policy-breaking instructions in content before that content influences an AI system.
whyExists: |
  AI systems increasingly ingest web pages, documents, emails, tickets, and tool outputs. Some of that content will contain instructions designed to override the system, leak data, or steer actions.
misuse: |
  It gets treated as a complete defence. Scanners can catch known patterns and suspicious language, but adversarial content adapts, and many attacks work by context rather than obvious wording.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means one layer in a wider safety design: content isolation, tool permissions, output checks, audit logs, least privilege, and careful handling of untrusted text.
