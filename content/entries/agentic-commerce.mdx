plainDefinition: |
  Agentic commerce describes buying flows where an AI agent can search, compare, negotiate, reserve, or purchase goods and services on behalf of a person or organisation.
whyExists: |
  Commerce systems were built around people browsing, clicking, and checking out. Agents change the shape of the flow: they need product data, constraints, permissions, payment authority, audit trails, and a clear moment where suggestion becomes transaction.
misuse: |
  The phrase is abused when ordinary personalisation, chat-based search, or affiliate recommendations are presented as delegated buying. A chatbot that says "you might like this" is not commerce with agency. It is merchandising with better manners.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means deciding what the agent may compare, which merchants it can contact, what budgets apply, how substitutions are approved, and who is accountable when the cheapest option was also the least survivable one.
