  The point at which the map stops being a map and starts auditioning for concierge work.
plainDefinition: |
  Ask Maps is Google's conversational Maps experience for asking natural-language questions about places, routes, and local constraints and getting map-aware recommendations back.
whyExists: |
  People do not think in tidy filter chips. They think in awkward constraints: chargers, queues, children, parking, weather, lighting, and whether the coffee is worth the detour. Ask Maps exists to make those messy requests legible to the product.
misuse: |
  It is described as local intelligence when it is still a synthesis layer built on listings, reviews, platform incentives, and model judgement. The interface becomes more humane. The uncertainty does not retire.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means conversational local search, quicker trip planning, reservation prompts, and one more surface where the user is encouraged to ask in prose while seeing less of the underlying source material directly.
