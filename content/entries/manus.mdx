  Manus is an agent product associated with multi-step task execution across browsing, research, and software tools, marketed as a system that can carry out longer chains of work than a standard chatbot.

  The appeal is obvious: people do not merely want better answers, they want finished tasks. A product that promises to browse, compare, draft, click, and return with the work already done sits directly on that desire.

  It gets treated as evidence that general-purpose autonomous agents are effectively solved. In reality, a polished demo reel, a waitlist, and a few successful runs do not abolish brittleness, supervision, or task selection.

  In practice it means a carefully packaged long-running agent workflow with browser actions, tool calls, and enough narrative polish to make ordinary automation feel like a historical turning point.
