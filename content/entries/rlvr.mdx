devilDefinition: |
  Teaching a model by only rewarding answers reality can mark for itself, which is wonderfully clean right up to the point where most useful work refuses to be that tidy.
plainDefinition: |
  RLVR stands for reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards. It is a post-training approach that improves model behaviour on tasks where success can be checked automatically, such as maths, code, or constrained reasoning problems.
whyExists: |
  Human preference data is expensive, inconsistent, and not always suited to questions with objectively checkable answers. RLVR exists because some domains offer a cleaner signal: the answer passes or it does not.
misuse: |
  It gets treated as though objective scoring automatically produces general intelligence, reliability, or wisdom. It does not. It works best where correctness can be verified, not where judgement, taste, or social context remain stubbornly involved.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means labs are squeezing better reasoning performance out of domains with tests, validators, or exact outcomes. The gains can be real. So is the temptation to pretend that a model good at verifiable tasks now understands everything else.
example: |
  "The RLVR gains were genuine, but they were still a poor substitute for checking whether the model could survive an ordinary human workflow."

RLVR is one of the few bits of current training jargon that deserves both scepticism and respect. The trick is remembering that a verifier is not a philosopher.
