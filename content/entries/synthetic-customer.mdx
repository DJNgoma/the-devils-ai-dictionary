devilDefinition: |
  A customer who never churns, never sues, and never says the thing that would have ended the meeting.
plainDefinition: |
  A synthetic customer is an AI-generated representation of a buyer, account, segment, or customer scenario used in testing, marketing, sales, support, or strategy work.
whyExists: |
  It exists because teams need quick ways to rehearse customer conversations, test positioning, explore support scenarios, and reason about segments before real market evidence is available or affordable.
misuse: |
  It gets abused when the generated customer becomes a witness for the team's assumptions. A synthetic customer can simulate a plausible reaction; it cannot create revenue, reveal private incentives, or punish a bad product by leaving.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it is useful for drafting sales objections, stress-testing support scripts, mapping journey assumptions, and finding questions for later research. It is weak as proof of demand, willingness to pay, churn risk, or strategic truth.
