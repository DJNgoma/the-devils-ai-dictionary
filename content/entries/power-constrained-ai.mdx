plainDefinition: |
  Power-constrained AI describes AI growth, deployment, or operations limited by electricity supply, grid capacity, substations, cooling, energy pricing, or the ability to place compute where enough power is available.
whyExists: |
  It exists because scaling AI is no longer only a question of better models and more chips. Dense accelerator clusters need dependable electricity, and the grid has its own release schedule.
misuse: |
  It is misused when power is treated as a temporary facilities nuisance or as a simple morality play. Constraints can involve transmission, peak demand, cooling, permitting, pricing, emissions targets, workload scheduling, and whether the proposed site can actually be connected.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means capacity planning, power purchase agreements, demand response, model and hardware efficiency, inference batching, workload placement, cooling design, queueing, and occasional humility in front of municipal infrastructure.
