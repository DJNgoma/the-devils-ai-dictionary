  A request that the AI budget produce useful work before the adoption dashboard produces another shade of green.
plainDefinition: |
  An AI scorecard is a proposed operating framework for measuring useful work, cost per successful task, dependability, and return on compute. It shifts attention from activity measures such as seats, prompts, tokens, or pilots towards outcomes that matter to users and the organisation.
whyExists: |
  AI spending can grow faster than evidence of value. A shared scorecard gives product, finance, engineering, risk, and operations a way to compare systems using completed work, reliability, cost, and business effect.
misuse: |
  A neat dashboard can make disputed definitions look settled. "Successful task" may hide rework, human review, displaced costs, safety failures, or work that was easy to count rather than useful to do.
practicalMeaning: |
  Define the task, success criteria, baseline, quality threshold, human effort, failure cost, and compute cost before deployment. Then measure outcomes over time and segment them by workflow instead of averaging every use case into one reassuring number.
example: |
  "The AI scorecard showed excellent adoption and modest useful work, a distinction the launch presentation had placed in the appendix."
