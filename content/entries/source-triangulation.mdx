devilDefinition: |
  Asking more than one source before letting the nearest confident page become reality.
plainDefinition: |
  Source triangulation is the practice of checking a claim across multiple independent sources to reduce reliance on a single potentially wrong, biased, stale, or derivative account.
whyExists: |
  AI research workflows are vulnerable to repeating one source's error, especially when other pages quote or summarise it. Triangulation helps separate independent evidence from an echo.
misuse: |
  It gets treated as counting links. Ten pages repeating the same press release are not ten sources; they are a chorus with syndication.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means comparing primary records, independent reporting, technical documentation, filings, datasets, or direct observations before presenting a claim as settled.
example: |
  "Source triangulation showed that the market-size number came from one consultancy slide and nine blogs with excellent copy-and-paste hygiene."
