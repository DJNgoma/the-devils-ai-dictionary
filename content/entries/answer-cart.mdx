plainDefinition: |
  An answer cart is an AI answer or recommendation surface that bundles suggested products, services, or actions into a path toward checkout, reservation, subscription, or purchase.
whyExists: |
  It exists because search, recommendation, and commerce are collapsing into the same interface. If the system can answer the user's question, vendors would prefer that answer to arrive with a basket, a button, and a commission structure.
misuse: |
  It gets presented as convenience while hiding incentives. An answer cart can reduce effort, but it can also blur the line between neutral recommendation, sponsored placement, affiliate economics, and delegated spending.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means product feeds, ranking rules, merchant eligibility, disclosure, substitution policy, budget limits, checkout handoff, and a way to tell whether the answer served the user or the margin.
