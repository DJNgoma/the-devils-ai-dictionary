plainDefinition: |
  SWE-Marathon is Abundant AI's benchmark of 20 multi-hour software-engineering tasks spanning library reproductions, full-stack product clones, machine-learning engineering, algorithmic work, and performance optimisation. It tests whether agents can sustain coherent progress over unusually long trajectories.
whyExists: |
  Many coding benchmarks reward short repository patches. Real engineering can require hours of design, implementation, testing, research, and recovery across a large deliverable, so SWE-Marathon extends the horizon and verifies the completed system rather than a single edit.
misuse: |
  A small task count is dismissed as anecdotal or a long runtime is mistaken for difficulty by itself. The tasks are deliberately expensive and heterogeneous; aggregate resolution rates need uncertainty, per-task evidence, harness details, and scrutiny of whether verifiers resist shortcuts.
practicalMeaning: |
  The benchmark publishes 20 executable environments, human reference solutions, multi-layer verification, and a large collection of trial trajectories. Its site reports roughly 1,400 logged trials and less than 26% resolution by frontier agents, making per-task behaviour as important as the headline average.
