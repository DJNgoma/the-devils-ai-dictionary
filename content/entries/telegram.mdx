devilDefinition: |
  A messaging app that periodically looks at the rest of the software industry and decides it would also like to be a platform, a payments layer, a bot host, and possibly a country.
plainDefinition: |
  Telegram is a messaging platform that is also a deployment surface for bots, automations, and Mini Apps. In AI contexts, it often serves as the conversational front end through which assistants, workflows, notifications, and lightweight products are delivered.
whyExists: |
  Because the easiest way to make software feel useful is often to place it where the user already is: inside the chat thread.
misuse: |
  By treating Telegram as just a messenger when the real value is often in its bot ecosystem, Mini App surface, lightweight UI layer, and low-friction interaction model. The platform question matters as much as the chat question.
practicalMeaning: |
  A practical interface layer for AI agents, internal tools, support flows, commerce, alerts, and small web apps that do not need the ceremony of a full native app. Telegram's own docs explicitly position bots and Mini Apps as capable of replacing websites, accepting payments, and handling business workflows.

Chat interfaces are powerful partly because they lower the activation energy for software. Telegram makes that especially obvious: a bot, a Mini App, and a payment layer can get surprisingly close to "product" before anyone has written a proper mobile app.
