devilDefinition: |
  A security architecture based on the radical proposition that the probabilistic intern should not hold the treasury keys.
plainDefinition: |
  Ledger Agent Stack is Ledger's architecture and toolkit for separating an agent's proposed action from secure authorisation. Agents produce intents; deterministic policies decide what may proceed; and high-risk or out-of-policy actions require human approval on separate secure hardware before signing.
whyExists: |
  Agents can search, negotiate, and prepare transactions quickly, but their probabilistic behaviour and exposure to malicious inputs make them poor custodians of private keys. Hardware-enforced policy creates a boundary the agent cannot rewrite through persuasion or prompt injection.
misuse: |
  Hardware signing is presented as complete agent governance. A secure signer can enforce the transaction it displays, but only if the intent is represented accurately, the policy is sound, the human understands the action, and surrounding identities, contracts, data, and off-chain effects are trustworthy.
practicalMeaning: |
  Organisations inventory agents and accountable owners, express proposed actions as structured intents, define policy in a hardware security boundary, and escalate exceptions for explicit signatures. They still need usable transaction displays, approval design, audit logs, recovery, and controls for actions that do not end in a cryptographic signature.
example: |
  "The agent proposed the transfer, the policy rejected it, and the hardware declined to be impressed by the agent's reasoning trace."
