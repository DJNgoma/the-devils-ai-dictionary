devilDefinition: |
  Solving a thinking problem by feeding the model more text until latency, cost, and coherence all lodge a complaint.
plainDefinition: |
  Context stuffing is the habit of overloading a model with documents, instructions, examples, and chat history in the hope that sheer volume will compensate for poor retrieval or unclear task design.
whyExists: |
  Because larger context windows make accumulation feel prudent, and because it is often easier to shovel everything in than to decide what actually matters.
misuse: |
  By using the phrase for any long prompt, even when the extra context is relevant and ranked well. The problem is not length alone. It is indiscriminate inclusion.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice context stuffing produces slower responses, higher bills, muddier instruction hierarchy, and outputs that feel informed in the broad sense while missing the one paragraph that mattered.
example: |
  "The agent had the entire handbook, six meeting transcripts, three policies, and still managed to ignore the refund rule sitting in perfect plain English."

Context stuffing is one of the first failure modes granted by abundance. Once the window grows large enough, restraint starts looking less like engineering and more like an avoidable meeting.
