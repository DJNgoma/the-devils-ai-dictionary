devilDefinition: |
  The condition in which every prompt is urgent, every action needs a click, and the human becomes a well-dressed Enter key.
plainDefinition: |
  Tool approval fatigue is the decline in human attention and judgement that happens when people are asked to approve too many agent tool calls, especially when the requests are repetitive, low-context, or poorly prioritised.
whyExists: |
  Human approval is a real control for risky actions. It fails when the workflow turns every minor step into an interruption and then acts surprised that the reviewer stops reading.
misuse: |
  Teams use approval prompts as proof that a human is in the loop. Often the human is only in the way. If the reviewer cannot distinguish trivial calls from dangerous ones, the approval surface becomes camouflage for weak automation design.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means risk-tiered approvals, batched requests, clear summaries, hard stops for irreversible actions, quiet handling for safe routine work, and escalation paths that respect the fact that vigilance is a scarce resource.
