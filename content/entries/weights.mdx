plainDefinition: |
  Weights are the learned numerical parameters inside a model. They are what the model ends up using to make predictions and generate outputs after it has been built.
whyExists: |
  Because a model is not merely an idea on paper. It becomes operational through the learned values that encode its statistical behaviour.
misuse: |
  By treating weights as synonymous with understanding, openness, or a complete product. Having access to weights matters, but it does not by itself explain data provenance, system quality, tooling, safety controls, or whether the thing belongs anywhere near production.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means portability, distribution, storage, deployment constraints, adaptation, and endless arguments over whether access to weights counts as openness or merely a different pricing model.
