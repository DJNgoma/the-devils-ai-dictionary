devilDefinition: |
  The attempt to persuade a prediction machine to share your intentions, your rules, and ideally your self-restraint.
plainDefinition: |
  Alignment refers to the work of making AI systems behave in ways that match intended goals, constraints, and human preferences rather than merely optimising for superficial reward signals.
whyExists: |
  Because specifying what humans actually want is hard, proxies are fragile, and capable systems can satisfy the letter of an objective while ignoring the reason it was written.
misuse: |
  By using it as a moral glow around any safety feature, or by collapsing very different concerns into one word. Product refusals, brand protection, catastrophic-risk research, and harmlessness tuning are related but not identical projects.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice alignment means a stack of compromises: data curation, system prompts, RLHF, policy rules, evals, red teaming, monitoring, and occasional reminders that users do not all want the same thing.
example: |
  "The model was described as aligned, which turned out to mean it refused bomb recipes, agreed with paying customers, and still found new ways to be oddly literal."

Alignment is less a solved property than an upkeep burden. The more capable the system becomes, the less wise it is to assume yesterday's controls still count as a durable settlement.
