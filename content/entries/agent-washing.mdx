devilDefinition: |
  Rebranding a workflow, chatbot, or macro as an agent so the budget sounds visionary instead of software-adjacent.
plainDefinition: |
  Agent washing is the practice of describing ordinary automation or LLM features as autonomous agents even when they show little real planning, tool use, memory, or independent decision-making.
whyExists: |
  Because "agent" currently signals strategic importance, investor appetite, and higher perceived value in a way that "workflow helper" regrettably does not.
misuse: |
  The term itself can also be overused to dismiss legitimate progress. Some systems do plan, call tools, recover from failure, and complete multi-step work. The problem is not the category. It is the inflation.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice agent washing usually means a renamed assistant, a brittle sequence of API calls, or a human-heavy service wrapped in the language of autonomy and sold at a more heroic multiple.
