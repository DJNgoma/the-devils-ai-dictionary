  Vercel is a cloud platform company best known for creating Next.js and for offering tightly integrated hosting, deployment, preview, and full-stack infrastructure around modern frontend frameworks. In current AI conversations, it also means products such as the AI SDK, AI Gateway, Sandbox, Workflow tooling, and a broader "AI Cloud" positioning around building AI-native web applications.

  Because developers like shipping quickly, preview environments are addictive, and framework-native deployment becomes commercially powerful once the framework itself shapes how the app is built.

  By talking about Vercel as if it were just "hosting" or, in the opposite direction, as if it were uniquely synonymous with the future of frontend. Vercel's strongest argument is not theoretical openness. It is that the product experience is often genuinely better when the framework and the platform are designed to flatter each other.

The tension, naturally, is that what feels like elegant integration to one team looks a lot like graceful captivity to another.
