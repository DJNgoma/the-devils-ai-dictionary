plainDefinition: |
  A permission prompt asks a user or operator to grant, deny, or scope access before a system performs an action or touches data, tools, files, accounts, or external services.
whyExists: |
  AI systems increasingly sit near sensitive capabilities: email, calendars, files, payments, code, customer records, and production tools. Permission prompts exist to slow down access and force an explicit decision at the boundary.
misuse: |
  They get overused until people click through them as a reflex, or under-specified until "allow" hides too much authority. A prompt that asks for broad access without explaining the concrete action is consent theatre with rounded corners.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means least-privilege design, clear action descriptions, revocable access, audit trails, sensible defaults, and enough friction that the agent cannot quietly turn convenience into a permissions estate.
example: |
  "The permission prompt said the agent wanted to help with email, which turned out to include reading the inbox, drafting replies, and developing opinions about procurement."
