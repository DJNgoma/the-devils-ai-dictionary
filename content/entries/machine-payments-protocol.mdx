plainDefinition: |
  Machine Payments Protocol is an open standard, co-authored by Tempo and Stripe, for paying inline with an HTTP request. A service can answer with `402 Payment Required`, after which the client supplies payment credentials and retries the request.
whyExists: |
  Machine clients often need API keys, billing accounts, or a human checkout flow before buying a small unit of service. MPP creates a standard challenge-and-payment exchange for usage-based APIs, agent transactions, and machine-to-machine commerce.
misuse: |
  It is presented as though a payment handshake makes an agent commercially trustworthy. The protocol can move money; it does not decide whether the purchase was authorised, sensible, reversible, or made by software anyone should have employed.
practicalMeaning: |
  A provider declares a price and accepted payment method in an HTTP challenge. The client chooses whether to pay, attaches proof, and retries. Teams still need spending limits, identity, receipts, dispute handling, and a policy for what the agent may buy before lunch.
