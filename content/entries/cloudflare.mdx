plainDefinition: |
  Cloudflare is an infrastructure and developer-platform company spanning networking, security, CDN, DNS, storage, databases, and edge/serverless compute. In developer and AI conversations, it most often means Workers, Pages, R2, Durable Objects, AI bindings, and a broader pitch built around global edge execution, integrated platform primitives, and anti-lock-in rhetoric.

  Because modern AI and web applications increasingly want three things at once: global distribution, programmable infrastructure, and fewer reasons to stitch together six vendors before lunch.

  By treating Cloudflare as either just a CDN or as a universal answer to every hosting argument. It is neither. It is a broad platform with real strengths in global distribution, integrated services, and cost posture, plus the usual trade-offs that come with choosing any large platform with strong opinions.

Cloudflare's current posture in the developer market is not subtle. It wants to be seen not merely as the place traffic passes through, but as the place modern apps, agents, and AI workloads actually live.

That matters for the Vercel rivalry. The argument is no longer just "whose hosting is faster?" It is "who gets to define the natural home of the modern full-stack app?"
