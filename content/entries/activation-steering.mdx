plainDefinition: |
  Activation steering is a control technique that adds or subtracts vectors in a model's activation space during inference to bias behaviour—style, refusal, or attributes—without changing weights permanently.
whyExists: |
  Researchers wanted test-time behavioural control finer than prompts and cheaper than fine-tunes. Activations became a knobs layer.
misuse: |
  Sold as reliable alignment. Steering can be brittle across prompts, models, and scales, and may trade one failure mode for another.
practicalMeaning: |
  Treat as an experimental control surface with eval suites. Prefer productised guardrails and policies for production unless you can measure stability.
