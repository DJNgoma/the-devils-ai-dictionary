  Cube is an analytics and semantic-layer platform. Its MCP server lets compatible AI clients connect to Cube so users can ask data questions and receive governed analytics context from the coding or assistant environment.
whyExists: |
  It exists because business data is full of definitions, joins, permissions, and metrics that do not belong inside a model's imagination. A semantic layer gives agents a governed way to ask questions against shared business logic.
misuse: |
  It gets treated as if connecting a model to analytics automatically creates trustworthy business intelligence. The quality still depends on the semantic model, access controls, freshness, metric definitions, and whether the question deserved a dashboard rather than a paragraph.

  In practice Cube means exposing governed metrics and analytics workflows to agents through a controlled interface, so data questions can use established definitions instead of freshly hallucinated SQL.
