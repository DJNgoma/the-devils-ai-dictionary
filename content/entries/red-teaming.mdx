devilDefinition: |
  The ritual of asking determined people to break the system before determined strangers do it for free.
plainDefinition: |
  Red teaming is adversarial testing aimed at finding harmful behaviours, security weaknesses, jailbreaks, unsafe tool interactions, or operational failures in an AI system.
whyExists: |
  Because benchmark scores, happy-path demos, and internal optimism do not cover what users, attackers, or merely curious employees will actually try once a system is live.
misuse: |
  By turning it into theatre: a one-off exercise, a slide in the launch deck, or a carefully controlled hunt for problems everyone already knows how to solve. Real red teaming is inconvenient precisely because it uncovers things the roadmap had hoped to ignore.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means structured adversarial prompts, tool-abuse scenarios, domain experts probing edge cases, and a repeatable process for turning discovered failures into mitigations rather than folklore.
example: |
  "The launch review improved once red teaming stopped being a ceremonial afternoon and started behaving like a serious attempt to embarrass the product."
