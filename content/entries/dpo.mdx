plainDefinition: |
  DPO (Direct Preference Optimisation) is a post-training method that aligns a language model to human preference pairs by optimising a classification-style objective, avoiding the separate reward-model and reinforcement-learning loop used in classic RLHF pipelines.
whyExists: |
  Preference alignment became central to making models usable, but full RLHF stacks are expensive, brittle, and operationally heavy. DPO offers a simpler path that still uses preferred-versus-rejected examples.
misuse: |
  It is treated as a free replacement for all of RLHF, evaluation, and governance. DPO still depends on preference data quality, coverage, and the base model. A clean algorithm does not purify a biased or gamed preference set.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means collecting or curating preference pairs, running a preference optimisation stage after supervised fine-tuning, and then measuring whether the model actually behaves better on the tasks and harms you care about — not only on the training pairs.
