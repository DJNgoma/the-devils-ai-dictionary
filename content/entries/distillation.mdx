devilDefinition: |
  The art of teaching a smaller model to imitate a larger one, then introducing the result as efficiency rather than inherited opinion.
plainDefinition: |
  Distillation is a training approach in which a smaller or cheaper model learns from the outputs, preferences, or intermediate signals of a larger model so it can reproduce some of its performance at lower cost.
whyExists: |
  It exists because frontier-grade behaviour is expensive to serve everywhere. If a smaller system can absorb enough of a stronger model's behaviour, products gain speed, lower inference cost, and a better shot at running locally or at scale.
misuse: |
  It is misused when people describe the resulting model as independent genius rather than compressed imitation. Distillation can preserve capability surprisingly well, but it also preserves blind spots, style, and hidden assumptions.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means generating a large synthetic training set from a stronger model, fine-tuning a cheaper one on that data, and accepting a careful trade-off between cost, latency, and capability.
