  The art of shrinking a heroic model into something finance can tolerate without product noticing too much.
plainDefinition: |
  Distillation is the practice of training a smaller model to imitate the outputs or behaviour of a larger model so it can serve similar tasks more cheaply or quickly.
whyExists: |
  Because frontier models are expensive to run, awkward to deploy, and frankly too grand for many production tasks that only need a narrower slice of their capability.
misuse: |
  By implying the smaller model is simply the same intelligence in a thriftier suit. Distillation can preserve a surprising amount, but it also preserves biases, omissions, and blind spots in more compact form.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means using a stronger model as a teacher so the cheaper one can handle a specific workflow with lower latency, lower cost, and fewer procurement speeches about GPU budgets.
example: |
  "The team called it distillation once the smaller model inherited enough of the original behaviour to keep support happy and infrastructure quiet."
