  The model family that made "open" a competitive move, a developer habit, and a licensing argument in one convenient package.
plainDefinition: |
  Llama is Meta's family of foundation models, released in multiple versions and sizes for research, development, and commercial use under terms that made the models widely deployable without making the conversation any less political.
whyExists: |
  Developers wanted strong deployable models outside closed APIs, and Meta wanted broad ecosystem influence, research visibility, and distribution through everyone else's hardware budget.
misuse: |
  It gets treated as a single model, as synonymous with open source, or as proof that downloading weights is the same thing as having deployment solved. "We use Llama" is usually the beginning of the sentence, not the useful part.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means evaluating a specific Llama variant for self-hosting, fine-tuning, or cost control, with all the usual adult questions about licences, throughput, memory, moderation, and whether the chosen size is actually fit for purpose.
