  Screen grounding is the process of linking an agent's visual understanding of a screen to the specific UI elements, coordinates, text, or controls it intends to act on.
whyExists: |
  Computer-use and browser agents need to turn pixels, screenshots, accessibility trees, and instructions into reliable actions. Grounding is the bridge between perception and control.
misuse: |
  It gets treated as solved because the model described the screen correctly. Describing a button and safely clicking the intended button are neighbouring skills, not twins.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means combining screenshots, accessibility metadata, element selectors, coordinates, confidence checks, and confirmation rules before important actions.
