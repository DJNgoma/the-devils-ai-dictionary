  Google's way of demonstrating that it, too, can participate in the open-model conversation without misplacing the rest of the product empire.
plainDefinition: |
  Gemma is Google's family of lighter-weight models released for developers to run, fine-tune, or adapt outside the company's main hosted product surfaces.
whyExists: |
  Many teams want smaller models for local use, private deployment, research, or fine-tuning. Google also benefits from having a developer-facing model family that can circulate beyond closed API products and keep it visible in the open-model ecosystem.
misuse: |
  It gets confused with Gemini, treated as a single model, or described as if "small" automatically means cheap, safe, or easy to deploy. As usual, the useful details live in the specific variant, size, licence terms, and workload.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice Gemma usually means a family of relatively lightweight Google models for experimentation, local inference, edge deployment, and fine-tuning work where a giant hosted model would be excessive, costly, or simply inconvenient.
