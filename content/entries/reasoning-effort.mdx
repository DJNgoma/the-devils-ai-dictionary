  The polite little dial that lets you spend extra time and money so the model can look more thoughtful before disappointing you with greater ceremony.
plainDefinition: |
  Reasoning effort is a model setting that asks a reasoning-capable model to spend more or less internal computation before answering, usually trading speed and cost for stronger performance on harder tasks.
whyExists: |
  It exists because not every prompt deserves the same amount of computational attention. A quick classification task and a multi-step coding problem should not be billed as though they require identical intellectual theatre.
misuse: |
  It is often treated as a truth slider. Higher effort can improve performance on difficult tasks, but it does not guarantee correctness, judgement, or freedom from the model's usual bad habits.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means deciding whether the task is worth the latency and token burn. Low effort is sensible for routine work. Higher effort is better reserved for coding, planning, maths, and the kinds of questions people later pretend were obvious.

  "They turned reasoning effort to high for a simple extraction job, which was a costly way to discover the value of restraint."
