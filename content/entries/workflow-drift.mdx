devilDefinition: |
  The slow change by which the workflow remains nominally the same while its actual behaviour acquires new shortcuts, new dependencies, and fresh ways to disappoint people.
plainDefinition: |
  Workflow drift is the change over time in how an end-to-end AI process behaves because its prompts, tools, routing, policies, operators, or surrounding systems evolve, even if the base model is unchanged.
whyExists: |
  Real workflows are layered systems. Tools get swapped, prompts accrete exceptions, queues change, approval rules soften, dependencies age, and humans learn workarounds. The term exists so teams can diagnose end-to-end change without blaming the model for every organisational improvisation.
misuse: |
  It becomes useless when it is treated as a vague synonym for decline. Sometimes the workflow has drifted. Sometimes the original design was flimsy, and production merely performed the courtesy of revealing it.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means traces that look subtly different month to month, new manual steps nobody documented, silent changes in handoff rules, degraded observability, or a workflow whose recovery logic now reflects three incidents and no clear architecture.
example: |
  "The model version had not moved in weeks. Workflow drift had, which is why the same task now required two extra tools, one apology, and a human rescue."

Workflow drift is what happens when systems acquire history faster than they acquire discipline. By the time someone names it, the workaround has usually already become tradition.
