devilDefinition: |
  A business card for software that lists its skills, endpoint, and preferred security scheme before asking another machine to delegate something important.
plainDefinition: |
  An Agent Card is an Agent2Agent metadata document that describes an agent's identity, endpoint, capabilities, skills, supported interaction modes, and security requirements. Other systems use it to discover how to communicate with the agent and what work it claims to accept.
whyExists: |
  Agent-to-agent delegation needs a discoverable contract before a task can be sent. The card gives clients a standard place to inspect an agent's advertised interface instead of relying on bespoke integration notes or hard-coded assumptions.
misuse: |
  A capability declaration is often treated as proof of capability. The card is self-description, not an evaluation, authorisation decision, uptime guarantee, or evidence that the agent will perform the advertised skill competently.
practicalMeaning: |
  A provider publishes the card, commonly at a well-known location, and keeps it aligned with the live service. Clients fetch it, validate its origin, select compatible methods, negotiate authentication, and apply their own trust policy before delegating work.
