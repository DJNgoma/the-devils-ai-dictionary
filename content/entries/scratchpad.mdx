plainDefinition: |
  A scratchpad is intermediate working memory—notes, partial plans, or chain-of-thought text—that a model or agent maintains while solving a task before emitting a final user-facing answer.
whyExists: |
  Complex tasks benefit from externalised intermediate state. Products needed a word for private reasoning channels separate from the polished response.
misuse: |
  Assumed to be truthful, complete, or safe to ignore. Hidden scratchpads can still leak via tools, logs, or side channels, and visible ones can be performative.
practicalMeaning: |
  Decide whether intermediates are logged, redacted, or discarded; do not put secrets in prompts if intermediates are stored. Use scratchpads as engineering state, not as a trust oracle.
