plainDefinition: |
  Delegated spending is the authority given to an AI agent or automated workflow to spend money, reserve paid services, or commit an organisation to financial obligations within defined rules.
whyExists: |
  Some useful agent work crosses a commercial boundary: booking a courier, buying media, reserving travel, renting compute, ordering parts, or renewing a subscription. Those actions need budgets and accountability, not just a clever prompt.
misuse: |
  It is abused when teams treat spending as a small extension of task automation. Buying is a side effect with external parties, contracts, tax records, fraud exposure, and occasionally a supplier who has learned to optimise for the robot buyer.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means budget envelopes, merchant controls, approval thresholds, exception queues, reconciliation, refund paths, and a way to explain why the agent chose speed, price, quality, or nonsense.
