  The legal and commercial discovery that a face is not free stock photography because a model can approximate it.
plainDefinition: |
  Likeness rights are the legal, contractual, and commercial interests a person may have in the use of their name, image, voice, performance, identity, or recognisable persona, especially in generated media.
whyExists: |
  They exist because faces, voices, names, and performances carry economic and personal value. AI makes imitation easier to scale, so old questions about permission, endorsement, compensation, and control arrive with cheaper tools and more plausible deniability.
misuse: |
  The term is misused when treated as a universal magic shield. Rights vary by jurisdiction, contract, labour context, publicity law, privacy law, and estate rules. Saying "likeness rights" does not settle who may train, generate, distribute, monetise, or withdraw.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means licences, release forms, union terms, creator contracts, territory limits, usage periods, consent records, compensation, attribution, model-training exclusions, takedown routes, and provenance systems that show what was used.
