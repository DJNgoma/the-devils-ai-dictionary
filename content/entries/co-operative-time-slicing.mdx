  Co-operative time-slicing is an llm-d scheduling approach that treats discrete reinforcement-learning steps, including rollout sampling and gradient training, as schedulable work. It interleaves steps from independent jobs on shared accelerators to reduce idle hardware between phases.
whyExists: |
  Reinforcement-learning post-training alternates between inference-heavy rollout generation and training-heavy parameter updates. Those phases use hardware differently and can stall while waiting for fresh data. Interleaving another job's compatible phase can raise accelerator utilisation without requiring each job to monopolise a cluster.
misuse: |
  It can sound like ordinary GPU sharing with a new hyphen. The co-operation depends on the RL workload exposing safe scheduling boundaries and the infrastructure coordinating different phases. Higher utilisation also does not guarantee faster completion for every individual job.
