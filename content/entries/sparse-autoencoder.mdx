plainDefinition: |
  A sparse autoencoder (SAE) is a model trained to reconstruct activations using a larger sparse feature basis, used in interpretability to find more monosemantic features than raw neurons provide.
whyExists: |
  Neurons are often polysemantic. SAEs offer a dictionary-learning approach to decompose activations into more interpretable features.
misuse: |
  Treated as complete explanations. Feature dictionaries can be incomplete, unstable, or hard to validate causally.
practicalMeaning: |
  Use SAEs as research instrumentation with causal checks. Do not claim full model understanding from a single dictionary.
