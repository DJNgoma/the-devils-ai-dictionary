devilDefinition: |
  The effort to keep a model's answer attached to evidence rather than to whatever polished sentence was statistically easiest to generate.
plainDefinition: |
  Grounding refers to tying a model's output to external evidence, context, or real-world signals so that the answer is based on something more reliable than the model's prior patterns alone.
whyExists: |
  Language models can sound certain even when they are improvising. Grounding exists to improve faithfulness by connecting answers to documents, databases, observations, or tool results that bear on the question.
misuse: |
  It gets used as a prestige synonym for "we attached some documents". That is not enough. If the evidence is irrelevant, stale, contradictory, or ignored by the prompt design, the system is not meaningfully grounded.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means retrieval, citation, tool outputs, or structured data being incorporated in a way that materially constrains the answer. Good grounding is less about adding more text and more about adding the right evidence with clear priority.
example: |
  "The grounded version of the assistant did not just answer more cautiously; it cited the actual policy entry it used and admitted when retrieval came back thin."
