  The practice of asking one stochastic witness whether another stochastic witness deserves a trophy.
plainDefinition: |
  LLM-as-a-judge is an evaluation pattern in which one language model scores, ranks, or compares outputs from another model against a rubric, preference signal, or pairwise choice.
whyExists: |
  Because human evaluation is expensive, slow, and inconveniently unwilling to scale at the pace product teams prefer.
misuse: |
  By treating the judge as neutral, objective, or magically aligned with user value. A model grader can inherit stylistic bias, provider bias, verbosity bias, and a remarkable affection for whatever sounds polished.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it is useful for triage, regression testing, and broad ranking, provided the rubric is clear and the results are anchored to human review on the cases that actually matter.

  "The model won the internal evals until an actual customer arrived and demonstrated that the judge had confused confidence with usefulness."
