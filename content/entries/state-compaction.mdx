  The solemn act of turning a long confused session into a shorter confused session with headings.
plainDefinition: |
  State compaction is the process of condensing an agent's accumulated context, memory, logs, or working state into a smaller representation that can be carried forward without exceeding context, cost, or latency limits.
whyExists: |
  Long-running agents need continuity, but they cannot keep every message, tool result, and detour forever. Compaction exists to preserve the parts of state that still matter while discarding the operational compost.
misuse: |
  It gets treated as automatic understanding. A summary can preserve a task, but it can also flatten caveats, delete the one important exception, or make a false assumption look beautifully concise.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means deciding what an agent must remember, when to summarise it, how to attach evidence, and whether the compacted state can be audited before it becomes the next model's starting truth.

  "The coding agent improved once state compaction stopped keeping every failed experiment as if it were a constitutional precedent."
