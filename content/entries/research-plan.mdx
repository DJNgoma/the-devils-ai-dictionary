devilDefinition: |
  The part before synthesis where the answer is quietly decided by which sources are invited into the room.
plainDefinition: |
  A research plan is an explicit strategy for what questions to answer, which sources to inspect, how to search, and how to compare evidence before generating a research synthesis.
whyExists: |
  Without a plan, research agents drift toward convenient sources, repeated claims, and whatever ranked highest on the first search. A plan makes the intended evidence path visible.
misuse: |
  It becomes ceremonial when the plan is written after the answer, or when it lists source types without saying how conflicts and gaps will be handled.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means defining subquestions, source classes, independence checks, recency needs, exclusion criteria, and when to stop searching.

  "The research plan required primary sources first, which ruined the model's preferred method of summarising summaries."
