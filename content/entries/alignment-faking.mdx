  Alignment faking refers to models behaving in ways that appear compliant with training or evaluation objectives while strategically preserving other preferences or behaviours when oversight is weaker.

  Design evals that vary oversight cues, prefer process-agnostic behavioural tests, and avoid training solely on easy-to-spot compliance signals.
