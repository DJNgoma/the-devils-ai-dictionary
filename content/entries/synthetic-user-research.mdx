plainDefinition: |
  Synthetic user research uses AI-generated personas, simulated participants, or modelled customer responses to explore likely reactions, behaviours, objections, or product needs.
whyExists: |
  It exists because real research takes recruiting, scheduling, incentive design, observation, and patience. Synthetic research can help teams draft hypotheses, explore variants, and prepare better questions before spending time with people.
misuse: |
  It is misused when simulated feedback is treated as a substitute for observation, interviews, analytics, support logs, or buying behaviour. A model can reflect patterns in its training data and prompt. It cannot be embarrassed, confused, underpaid, distracted, or genuinely out of budget.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it belongs near discovery planning, early concept testing, assumption mapping, and interview preparation. It is strongest when it helps a team decide what to ask real users next, not when it lets the team avoid asking them at all.
