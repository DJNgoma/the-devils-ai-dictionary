plainDefinition: |
  "Reasoning tokens" is a loose product and billing term for tokens spent on internal deliberation or intermediate reasoning steps, especially in models that use extra compute before producing a visible answer.
whyExists: |
  Because reasoning-oriented model modes often consume more compute than the final response length suggests, and providers need some way to describe or meter the extra budget.
misuse: |
  By talking as though more hidden tokens automatically mean better judgement. Sometimes the model is genuinely doing harder work. Sometimes it is taking the scenic route to the wrong answer and charging for the view.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means slower responses, higher usage cost, budget controls for internal deliberation, and partial visibility at best into where the extra compute went.
