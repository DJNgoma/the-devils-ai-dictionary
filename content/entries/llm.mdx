  LLM stands for large language model: a model trained to predict and generate language, often across many tasks involving text, code, and increasingly tools and multimodal inputs.
whyExists: |
  The field needed a short label for the class of large generative models that sit underneath much of the current AI boom. The acronym is easier to say than the architecture, which is part of its success.
misuse: |
  By using LLM as a synonym for AI, intelligence, or product value. An LLM can be the engine inside a useful system, but the acronym by itself explains very little about whether the result is accurate, safe, or worth paying for.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means a flexible language-and-code engine that can draft, classify, summarise, translate, search, and bluff, depending on how it is prompted, constrained, and surrounded by everything else.
