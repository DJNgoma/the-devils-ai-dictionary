  A dataset in which the people being represented are permitted the radical luxury of describing themselves.
plainDefinition: |
  A community library is a community-owned collection of consented images or videos, descriptions, themes, and ratings that records how a group wants to be represented in AI training and evaluation. Microsoft Research's Community Library Creator provides a structured process for building one.
whyExists: |
  Internet-scale datasets can reproduce stereotypes, omit ordinary life, and deny represented people control over their data. Participatory curation adds lived context and lets communities define examples and evaluation criteria for more accurate representation.
misuse: |
  The term is mistaken for a public folder of diverse images, a synthetic-data collection, or proof that bias has been solved. A library represents the participants and choices that produced it; ownership, consent, disagreement, coverage, and downstream use remain live questions.
practicalMeaning: |
  An advocacy organisation helps members choose themes, collect and describe material, review generated outputs, and set sharing conditions. The community owns the library, can limit access, and can remove contributed data; developers must preserve those terms and test what the model actually learns.
