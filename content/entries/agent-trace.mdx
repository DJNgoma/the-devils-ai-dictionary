devilDefinition: |
  The breadcrumb trail left by software that was meant to act independently and then needed a chaperone with a notebook.
plainDefinition: |
  An agent trace is a structured record of the steps an agent took during a run, including prompts, model responses, tool calls, observations, decisions, approvals, errors, and final outputs.
whyExists: |
  Multi-step agent runs are hard to judge from the final answer alone. The trace gives reviewers a way to see whether the agent followed a sensible path, called the right tools, respected boundaries, and recovered from problems instead of accidentally succeeding.
misuse: |
  It gets treated as proof of reasoning rather than evidence of process. A trace can be incomplete, selectively logged, or polished by the platform. It shows what was recorded, not necessarily every cause behind the behaviour.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means a timeline that developers, auditors, or operators can replay when a workflow fails, costs too much, calls the wrong tool, or produces an answer no one wants to defend from memory.
