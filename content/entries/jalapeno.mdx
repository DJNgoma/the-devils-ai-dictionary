plainDefinition: |
  Jalapeño is a custom inference chip developed by OpenAI and Broadcom for serving large language models. It represents a move towards silicon designed around a frontier lab's own inference workloads rather than relying only on general AI accelerators.
whyExists: |
  At large scale, inference cost, power use, memory movement, latency, and supply become strategic constraints. Custom silicon can optimise those trade-offs around a known model stack and reduce dependence on a single accelerator market.
misuse: |
  A custom chip is treated as automatic proof of lower costs or technical independence. Its value depends on software support, yields, packaging, memory, networking, utilisation, deployment volume, and whether the models fit the assumptions built into the hardware.
practicalMeaning: |
  The chip matters less as a benchmark trophy than as a signal about vertical integration. Procurement and infrastructure teams should ask what workloads it serves, where it is deployed, what stack supports it, and whether claimed savings survive full-system costs.
