devilDefinition: |
  The comforting belief that every user click is secretly training a moat, provided nobody asks about consent, quality, or storage.
plainDefinition: |
  A data flywheel is a feedback loop where product usage creates data that can improve models, features, personalisation, evaluation, or operations, which then attracts more usage and produces more data.
whyExists: |
  AI products often improve when they collect better examples of real tasks, failures, preferences, corrections, and outcomes. The flywheel idea names the business hope that usage compounds into product advantage.
misuse: |
  It gets invoked whenever a company has users and a database. Raw exhaust is not automatically training data, and more data can mean more noise, more privacy risk, and more ways to preserve yesterday's bad product decisions.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means instrumentation, consent boundaries, data retention rules, labelling workflows, eval sets, privacy review, and a sober answer to whether the collected signal is actually useful for improving anything.
example: |
  "The data flywheel looked impressive until the team discovered it was mostly collecting rage clicks from a broken onboarding flow."
