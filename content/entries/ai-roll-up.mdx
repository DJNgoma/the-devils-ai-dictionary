devilDefinition: |
  The theory that buying a normal business becomes innovation once the margin plan learns to say "agentic".
plainDefinition: |
  An AI roll-up is a strategy in which operators or investors acquire existing businesses and try to improve profitability or scale by standardising processes, adding software, and using AI to automate parts of service delivery or operations.
whyExists: |
  It exists because many industries remain fragmented, process-heavy, and badly digitised. That creates a tempting thesis: buy the businesses, modernise the workflows, and collect the efficiency gains before anyone else learns to describe them as inevitable.
misuse: |
  The term is misused when people speak as if labour were just software waiting for courage. Acquired businesses are full of local habits, tacit knowledge, regulation, customer trust, and operational ugliness that does not vanish because a deck says automation.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means some real workflow improvement, some software unification, some headcount pressure, and a great many founders discovering that regional service businesses have more edge cases than a prompt can politely absorb.
