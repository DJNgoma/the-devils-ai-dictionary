  The ceremony in which a workflow acquires signatures, screenshots, and solemn nods without acquiring much resistance.
plainDefinition: |
  Approval theatre is a performative approval process that creates the appearance of oversight without materially changing who can act, what gets checked, or whether the risky thing can be stopped.
whyExists: |
  Organisations genuinely need review and accountability. Under delivery pressure, though, it is temptingly easy to build approval surfaces that reassure executives, auditors, or product teams without giving reviewers enough evidence or authority to matter.
misuse: |
  Not every lightweight approval path is theatre. Fast approvals can still be real if the reviewer sees the right evidence and can meaningfully block the action. The phrase earns its keep when the approval is decorative, rushed, or structurally toothless.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means unread dashboards, one-click sign-offs, ritual thumbs-up messages, reviewers with no context, and nominal gates that cannot stop the system because the incentives, access, or deadlines have already decided the outcome.

Approval theatre is what happens when the approval artefact matters more than the approval decision. The paperwork flourishes precisely as the control weakens.
