  Cryptographic attestation that a weight file is the one you meant to deploy, not a surprise remix from an untrusted mirror.
plainDefinition: |
  Model signing attaches cryptographic signatures or attestations to model artefacts so deployers can verify integrity and authenticity before loading weights into production.
whyExists: |
  Weights are large binaries moved across registries and clouds. Integrity checks reduce tampering and mix-up risk.
misuse: |
  Treated as proof the model is safe or unbiased. Signatures prove provenance of bits, not goodness of behaviour.
practicalMeaning: |
  Sign at publish time, verify at deploy time, manage keys properly, and fail closed on mismatch.

  "Model signing blocked a checkpoint that had the right name and the wrong hash."
