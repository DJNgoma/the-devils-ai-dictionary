  Lucy 2.5 is Decart's 720p real-time video-editing model. It transforms a live or streamed video using a text prompt, a reference image, or both, and can add, replace, or remove objects, swap characters and backgrounds, change clothing, and apply styles or effects.

  Conventional generative video usually produces a clip after a wait. Interactive filters, virtual try-on, live shopping, games, and broadcast effects need output quickly enough to remain part of the live experience.

  "Real-time editing" can sound like ordinary deterministic compositing with better prompts. The model is generating altered frames under latency, consistency, identity, and motion constraints; a convincing short demonstration does not guarantee a stable long session.

  Developers connect a video stream through Decart's SDK or real-time API, select `lucy-2.5`, and update prompts or reference images during the session. They still need to test delay, frame stability, reference adherence, safety, consent, bandwidth, cost, and failure behaviour on their actual cameras and subjects.
