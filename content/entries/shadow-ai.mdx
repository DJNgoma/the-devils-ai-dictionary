  Shadow AI refers to the unapproved or unsanctioned use of AI tools, models, or automations inside an organisation, often by staff trying to solve real problems faster than formal procurement or governance allows.

  Because demand for useful tools appears before policy, approved platforms arrive late, and workers rarely stop needing help just because the risk committee has booked a workshop.

  By treating all unsanctioned use as either criminal recklessness or heroic innovation. Most shadow AI is neither. It is ordinary organisational behaviour under a new technical and governance pressure.

  In practice it usually means staff pasting documents into public chatbots, expensing niche AI tools, wiring up personal automations, or quietly using consumer products for work tasks that official systems still handle badly.

Shadow AI is often less a rebellion than a timing problem. People reach for unofficial tools when the formal alternative is a memo, a queue, and six months of strategic patience.
