devilDefinition: |
  The distance between what the dashboard says was tested and what the product can actually do in public.
plainDefinition: |
  Eval coverage describes which behaviours, tasks, user groups, risk areas, languages, tools, and failure modes are actually represented in an evaluation suite.
whyExists: |
  Evaluation results are only meaningful in relation to what was tested. Coverage makes the boundaries of confidence visible.
misuse: |
  It gets hidden behind aggregate scores. A high pass rate says little if the eval suite avoids hard cases, rare users, dangerous tools, or the languages where the product is being sold.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means mapping product capabilities and risks to tests, identifying blind spots, and reporting gaps with the same seriousness as pass rates.
