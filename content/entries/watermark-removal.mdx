  Watermark removal is the attempt to strip, degrade, obscure, crop, transform, or otherwise defeat a visible or invisible marker used to identify generated, edited, or protected content.
whyExists: |
  Watermarks and provenance markers are used for attribution, disclosure, ownership, and synthetic-content signalling. Attackers, opportunists, and ordinary reposting workflows may remove or damage them.
misuse: |
  It gets discussed as though only malicious experts do it. Compression, screenshots, re-encoding, cropping, platform transforms, and casual editing can all weaken provenance signals.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means watermarking is one layer, not a complete trust system. Detection, provenance metadata, platform policy, and content analysis still matter.
