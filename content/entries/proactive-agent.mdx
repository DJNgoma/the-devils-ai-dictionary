plainDefinition: |
  A proactive agent is an AI system designed to initiate actions, suggestions, or workflows without waiting for a fresh user turn, usually based on schedules, events, monitored context, or prior goals.
whyExists: |
  Many valuable tasks arrive as reminders, status changes, deadlines, and half-finished work rather than as neat chat prompts. Product teams want agents that notice and move, not only answer.
misuse: |
  Proactivity is sold as intelligence. Without policy, budgets, interruption design, and clear stop conditions, it becomes spam with write access. Acting first is easy; acting correctly without supervision is the hard part.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means defining triggers, permission scopes, spend limits, notification rules, approval gates for irreversible steps, and a way for users to see why the agent woke up and how to put it back to sleep.
example: |
  "The proactive agent booked the follow-up, rewrote the brief, and only then asked whether anyone still wanted the meeting."
