plainDefinition: |
  Context compaction is the process of summarising, compressing, or otherwise reducing accumulated working context so an AI session can continue within a context window or operational budget.
whyExists: |
  Long-running agents and assistants accumulate instructions, tool results, decisions, and user preferences. Compaction keeps the session usable when the raw transcript would exceed limits or become too costly to carry forward.
misuse: |
  It gets treated as lossless memory. It is not. Compaction preserves a judgement about what mattered, and that judgement can omit caveats, uncertainty, failed attempts, and inconvenient constraints.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means deciding what to keep, what to discard, how to mark uncertainty, and how to recover original evidence when the compacted version starts behaving like an unreliable witness.
