devilDefinition: |
  What happens when every pilot leaves behind an embedding index, a bill, and nobody willing to call it production.
plainDefinition: |
  Vector sprawl is the uncontrolled growth of embedding indexes, vector databases, duplicate corpora, stale chunks, and retrieval experiments across an organisation.
whyExists: |
  Embeddings make prototypes easy to start. They also make it easy to create overlapping indexes with unclear owners, inconsistent refresh schedules, forgotten permissions, and retrieval behaviour no one can compare.
misuse: |
  Teams treat it as a storage tidy-up problem when it is usually an ownership, governance, cost, and quality problem. Deleting a vector database is simple; knowing whether anything important depends on it is the invoice.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means inventorying indexes, documenting corpora and embedding models, deduplicating sources, enforcing retention and access rules, measuring retrieval quality, and retiring experiments that have quietly become infrastructure.

  "The AI programme paused for vector sprawl remediation after finance found six indexes of the same policies disagreeing at different prices."
