devilDefinition: |
  A licence unit invented when software became staff-shaped and vendors remembered how much they missed headcount pricing.
plainDefinition: |
  An agent seat is a pricing or licensing unit that treats an AI agent, digital worker, or autonomous workflow as a billable seat rather than merely a feature, API call, or usage tier.
whyExists: |
  Vendors need a way to charge for software that performs role-like work across teams or systems. Buyers also need a way to compare the cost of an agent against staff, outsourcing, usage fees, and the unpriced labour of supervising it.
misuse: |
  It is abused when a seat implies dependable labour capacity that the product has not earned. A paid agent seat may still be a bundle of workflows, model calls, approvals, and support tickets wearing a small badge that says "colleague".
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means procurement must ask what counts as one agent, how many workflows it can run, which users may supervise it, what usage is included, and whether the vendor has simply found a more flattering word for quota.
