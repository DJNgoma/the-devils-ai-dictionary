  Workflow software reintroduced to management as headcount, because procurement enjoys a dramatic entrance.
plainDefinition: |
  "Digital worker" is a vendor phrase for automation software, often AI-assisted, that is sold as though it were a bounded employee capable of handling routine business tasks across systems.
whyExists: |
  Because buyers understand roles more quickly than architectures, and vendors would rather sell "an accounts payable worker" than "a model-mediated process layer with brittle integrations".
misuse: |
  By speaking as if software has judgement, accountability, or broad role competence simply because it can complete a workflow. Calling it a worker does not make it adaptable, trustworthy, or employable in the human sense.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means a mix of rules, integrations, model calls, approval steps, and exception handling wrapped in labour metaphors so the budget can be compared to a salary instead of a software line item.

  "The demo promised a digital worker for claims processing. The implementation delivered an approval queue with a more theatrical org chart."
