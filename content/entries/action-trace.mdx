  The receipt for what the system did, for those awkward moments when the final answer says everything went smoothly.
plainDefinition: |
  An action trace is a structured record of external actions an AI system or agent took, including tool calls, browser actions, approvals, inputs, outputs, and resulting state changes.
whyExists: |
  Agents increasingly do things outside the chat box. Teams need a way to audit, debug, replay, and explain those actions after the fact.
misuse: |
  It gets reduced to a transcript. A useful action trace must capture the action, context, authority, result, and side effect, not merely the sentence the assistant wrote afterwards.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means logging enough detail to answer who authorised what, which tool or UI element was touched, what changed, and how to reverse or investigate the action.
example: |
  "The action trace showed the assistant submitted the form after the approval gate, which made the apology shorter and the fix more obvious."
