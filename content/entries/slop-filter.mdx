  It exists because generative output is now cheap enough to overwhelm the surfaces that were built for scarcer human effort. Someone has to decide which nearly acceptable thing still deserves to be ignored.

  It gets oversold as a clean detector for "AI content". The harder job is not identifying whether a model helped, but deciding whether the result is useful, truthful, original, relevant, and worth a human's attention.

  In practice it means classifiers, provenance signals, editorial review, spam rules, ranking demotion, appeal paths, and the awkward admission that platforms now need sanitation for their own growth strategy.
