devilDefinition: |
  A voice model fast enough to interrupt naturally and polished enough to make the interruption feel like a feature.
plainDefinition: |
  GPT-Live is OpenAI's generation of real-time voice models used for continuous spoken interaction, including ChatGPT Voice. It is designed to handle speech directly with low enough latency for conversation rather than treating voice as a slow exchange of recorded turns.
whyExists: |
  Natural conversation depends on timing, interruption, tone, pacing, and rapid response. A live speech system aims to make those behaviours part of the model interaction instead of stitching together a transcript, text model, and separate voice after each turn.
misuse: |
  "Real-time" is taken to mean instantaneous, human, or safe in every language and acoustic setting. Latency, turn detection, accents, emotion, hallucinations, network quality, and tool delays still determine whether the conversation works.
practicalMeaning: |
  Product teams gain a more fluid spoken interface but must test end-to-end delay, barge-in behaviour, audio quality, transcription or logging policy, disclosure, accessibility, tool-call timing, and graceful fallback under poor network conditions.
