  A concierge service for accelerators, provided the concierge also owns part of the waiting list.
plainDefinition: |
  A GPU broker is an intermediary that helps organisations find, reserve, lease, or buy access to GPU capacity across clouds, neoclouds, data centres, private clusters, or specialised compute marketplaces.
whyExists: |
  It exists because demand for accelerators is uneven, expensive, and geographically constrained. Buyers often need capacity faster than ordinary procurement or cloud quotas can provide it, while sellers need committed utilisation for hardware that does not enjoy being idle.
misuse: |
  It is misused when brokering is treated as a compute strategy. A broker can find supply, but does not magically remove cluster reliability, data movement, compliance, support quality, quota limits, contract lock-in, or the awkward fact that everyone else also wanted the same chips.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means checking availability windows, GPU type, region, network performance, storage, support terms, minimum commitments, cancellation rights, egress costs, security posture, and whether the quoted capacity exists outside the spreadsheet.
