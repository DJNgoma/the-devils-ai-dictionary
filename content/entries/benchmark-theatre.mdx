  The ritual of treating a tidy score as proof that your messy product has become reliable.

  The practice of using selective benchmark results, public leaderboards, or scripted evaluations as marketing theatre that outruns operational reality.

  Because people need compressed ways to compare systems, and benchmarks are useful when they are honest about scope. The trouble starts when a score becomes a substitute for product evidence.
misuse: |
  By presenting benchmark wins as if they settle questions about usability, cost, robustness, or risk. They do not. They usually settle only the question of who curated the exam.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means a product story with stronger charts than customer evidence, and a team quietly hoping nobody asks what happens outside the test harness.
