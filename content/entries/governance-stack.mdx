  The layered arrangement of policies, approvals, logs, and enforcement mechanisms that determines whether the organisation has governance or merely a decorative fondness for the word.
plainDefinition: |
  Governance stack is the combination of policies, review gates, runtime controls, audit logs, escalation paths, and accountability mechanisms used to keep an AI system bounded and answerable in operation.
whyExists: |
  No single control is enough. Policy without enforcement is sentiment. Logs without ownership are archaeology. Approvals without evidence are theatre. The term exists to name the fact that governance is a layered system rather than a solitary document or committee.
misuse: |
  It gets spoken about as if more layers automatically mean more safety. A bloated stack can still be toothless if the controls do not connect, the owners cannot act, or the commercial incentives remain free to ignore the whole arrangement.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means policy rules, tool permissions, approval flows, release gates, monitoring, incident paths, and clear authority over who can pause, roll back, or refuse a system when the risk story starts sounding imaginative.
