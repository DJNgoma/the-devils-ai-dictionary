devilDefinition: |
  The claim that access to the same APIs as everyone else has somehow produced a castle.
plainDefinition: |
  An AI moat is the supposed durable competitive advantage a company claims to gain from its models, data, distribution, workflow position, cost structure, or proprietary deployment know-how.
whyExists: |
  Because investors and operators need a way to distinguish temporary novelty from lasting advantage. In AI markets, model capability moves quickly, so the question of what remains defensible arrives earlier and less politely.
misuse: |
  By implying that mere model access is itself a moat. Using a frontier API is not a moat. Adding chat to a familiar workflow is not automatically a moat either. Sometimes the only durable thing in the room is the slide about defensibility.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice a real moat usually lives in distribution, embedded workflow, proprietary data feedback loops, operational excellence, trust, switching costs, or an ability to make the economics work after the demo glow fades.
example: |
  "Their AI moat turned out to be a paid API account and an unusually ornate seed deck."
