devilDefinition: |
  The compromise term for when a company wants the glow of openness without the administrative burden of actually being open source.
plainDefinition: |
  An open-weight model is one whose trained parameters are released for others to download and run, even if the training code, data, licences, or usage rights remain restricted.
whyExists: |
  The term exists because there is a meaningful difference between sharing a model's weights and sharing the full ingredients behind it. The industry needed language for that middle ground, if only to avoid calling everything open source with a straight face.
misuse: |
  It is misused whenever "open weight" is presented as equivalent to full transparency, unrestricted reuse, or community governance. A downloadable checkpoint can still arrive with significant legal, technical, and commercial strings attached.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means you can run or fine-tune the model yourself, but you may not know precisely how it was trained, what data shaped it, or what terms govern your most lucrative use case.
