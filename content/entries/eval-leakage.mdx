plainDefinition: |
  Eval leakage is the contamination of an evaluation by training data, tuning loops, prompt iteration, or repeated rehearsal in ways that make measured performance look more general than it really is.
whyExists: |
  The term exists because AI systems are improved by repeated exposure to tasks, examples, and feedback. Without a name for leakage, teams can mistake familiarity with the test for competence on the real job.
misuse: |
  It gets invoked every time a benchmark result looks strong. Not every good score is leakage. The phrase is useful when the separation between training and evaluation has genuinely weakened, not when someone merely dislikes the number.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means guarding holdouts, rotating fresh tasks, avoiding public overexposure of narrow eval sets, and checking whether the optimisation loop has quietly learned the taste of the exam instead of the substance of the task.

Eval leakage is not a moral category. It is a measurement problem with career consequences, which is why people keep trying to discuss it as if it were merely philosophical.
