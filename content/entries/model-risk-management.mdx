plainDefinition: |
  Model risk management is the set of governance, validation, monitoring, documentation, and control practices used to identify and manage risks created by models in business processes.
whyExists: |
  Models can fail through bias, drift, misuse, bad data, overconfidence, hidden assumptions, weak validation, unclear ownership, and operational dependence. AI has made those risks broader, faster, and harder to keep politely inside spreadsheets.
misuse: |
  It is misused when teams equate risk management with a launch checklist or a policy document. The useful version follows the model through design, data, testing, approval, deployment, monitoring, change control, and retirement.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means inventories, risk tiering, independent validation, evaluation coverage, monitoring, incident handling, version control, human oversight, documentation, and clear accountability when model behaviour affects money, rights, safety, or operations.
