plainDefinition: |
  RLHF stands for reinforcement learning from human feedback. Broadly, it refers to techniques in which human preference data or judgements are used to shape a model's behaviour, often by training a reward model and then optimising the model toward higher-scoring outputs.
whyExists: |
  Pretrained models are not automatically aligned with product expectations or human preferences. RLHF exists to steer behaviour toward responses people judge as more useful, safer, or more on-policy.
misuse: |
  It is often used as a fuzzy synonym for "we made the model nicer". That misses the actual mechanics and trade-offs. Preference optimisation can improve behaviour, but it can also encourage blandness, sycophancy, or reward-hacking if the signal is weak or narrow.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means collecting preference judgements, training systems around those judgements, and accepting that you are operationalising a particular slice of human preference rather than discovering universal virtue.
