plainDefinition: |
  Agent identity is the distinct identity assigned to an AI agent or automated actor so its permissions, actions, ownership, and audit trail can be managed separately from a human user or generic service account.
whyExists: |
  Agents increasingly act across systems, call tools, request data, and trigger side effects. Giving them a recognisable identity lets teams scope access, attribute actions, rotate credentials, review behaviour, and shut down the right actor when something goes wrong.
misuse: |
  It gets confused with branding or personality. Naming an assistant "Milo" is not identity management. The useful question is whether systems can enforce what Milo may touch and prove what Milo actually did.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means non-human principals, scoped roles, owner metadata, credential lifecycle, run-level attribution, policy checks, and logs that do not collapse the agent into whichever user happened to approve the first prompt.
