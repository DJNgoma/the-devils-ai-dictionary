  The flattering phrase for a system that appears to know enough structure for humans to start projecting philosophy onto it.
plainDefinition: |
  A world model is an internal representation that helps a system predict how states, objects, actions, or events relate to one another over time.
whyExists: |
  The term exists because systems that can anticipate structure, consequence, and continuity often perform better than systems that merely react to the next local cue.
misuse: |
  By treating any evidence of internal structure as proof of deep understanding, common sense, or machine comprehension of life itself. The phrase is often used at exactly the point where caution should become more fashionable.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means a model has learned enough regularity to predict useful consequences, not that it has joined the human condition. It is a technical claim that acquires mystical overtones the moment it leaves the lab.
