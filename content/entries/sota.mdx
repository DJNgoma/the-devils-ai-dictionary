  The temporary throne awarded to whichever model most recently aced the exam everyone has already started teaching to.
plainDefinition: |
  SOTA, short for "state of the art", means the best reported result on a benchmark, task, or capability at a given moment.
whyExists: |
  The term exists because research and product teams need shorthand for comparative progress, especially when models, methods, or systems improve quickly enough that normal adjectives feel tired by Wednesday.
misuse: |
  It is misused whenever benchmark leadership is treated as proof of broad intelligence, product quality, or durable advantage. Being first on a scoreboard can mean many things, including genuine progress and aggressive test preparation.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means marketing leverage, recruiting leverage, investor leverage, and a strong incentive to optimise for published comparisons even when customers care more about price, latency, reliability, and integration.
example: |
  "The model was SOTA on paper and unexpectedly ordinary in production, which is a very popular genre of achievement."
