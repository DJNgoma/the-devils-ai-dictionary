devilDefinition: |
  The stage of the transformation programme where every team acquires its own obedient little machine and nobody acquires a map.
plainDefinition: |
  Agent sprawl is the uncontrolled proliferation of agents, automations, copilots, or assistant workflows across an organisation without clear ownership, boundaries, governance, or a sober account of overlap.
whyExists: |
  Agent tooling has become easier to launch than to govern. Local teams can spin up useful workflows quickly, and those small wins accumulate into a larger estate of permissions, prompts, budgets, and duplicated behaviours that nobody intended to administer as a single system.
misuse: |
  It gets used to sneer at any experimentation at all. Some multiplication is healthy. Sprawl begins when the overlap, risk, and maintenance burden outgrow the visibility and discipline around them.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means duplicate agents answering the same question differently, unclear ownership when one breaks, overlapping permissions, rising spend, inconsistent policy handling, and a governance meeting that discovers the estate only after the estate has discovered payroll.
