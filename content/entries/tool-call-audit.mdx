  A tool call audit is a reviewable record of tool invocations made by a model or agent, including arguments, results, timing, caller identity, permissions, and side effects.
whyExists: |
  Tool use moves AI systems from text generation into action. Once a model can query data, write files, send messages, or change records, the organisation needs a way to inspect what was called and what happened next.
misuse: |
  It gets reduced to raw logs nobody can interpret. A useful audit does not merely prove that an endpoint was hit. It shows whether the call was authorised, expected, correctly formed, successful, and tied to an accountable workflow.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means structured logs, argument capture, result summaries, redaction policy, actor identity, permission state, idempotency markers, and enough retention to investigate incidents without storing every secret forever.
