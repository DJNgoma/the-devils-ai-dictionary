devilDefinition: |
  The switchboard that promises the right model for every job and occasionally adds a thrilling new place for policy mistakes to hide.
plainDefinition: |
  A model router is a software layer that selects among multiple models or providers for a given request based on rules or signals such as task type, latency, cost, availability, policy, or fallback behaviour.
whyExists: |
  Because no single model is best at everything, outages happen, prices move, and product teams would prefer not to hard-code every bet into the application layer.
misuse: |
  By marketing routing itself as intelligence, or by implying that a multi-model stack is automatically well evaluated. A router can express good operational judgement. It can also just spread uncertainty across more vendors.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means heuristics, policy rules, retries, provider abstractions, observability, and a permanent negotiation between quality, speed, and the monthly bill.
example: |
  "The model router kept the app online during the outage, then introduced a fresh round of debugging because two providers interpreted the same tool schema differently."
