plainDefinition: |
  Capability escalation is the process by which an AI system, agent, or workflow gains access to stronger tools, wider data, higher authority, or more consequential actions.
whyExists: |
  Some tasks genuinely require more capability than the initial environment allows. Escalation lets systems request or receive additional powers when the task, user, or approval path justifies them.
misuse: |
  It gets hidden inside smooth product flows, where a harmless assistant gradually acquires write access, external actions, payment authority, or sensitive data because nobody wanted to interrupt the demo.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means approval gates, permission prompts, scoped credentials, audit trails, rollback paths, and a bias toward granting the smallest new capability that solves the task.
