  Test-time compute is the extra computation spent during inference to improve task performance, often through additional reasoning steps, search, sampling, verification, or tool use.

  Because some problems are not solved by one cheap pass. Harder tasks can benefit when the system is allowed to spend more time and compute before committing to an answer.

  By talking as if extra compute were a direct synonym for intelligence. It can improve performance on the right tasks, but it can also produce a slower path to the same wrong conclusion at a more mature price point.

  In practice it usually means higher latency, higher token usage, and a larger invoice in exchange for better odds on structured or difficult tasks where additional search or verification actually helps.
