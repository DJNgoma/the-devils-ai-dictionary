plainDefinition: |
  Synthetic media is image, audio, video, or multimodal content generated or substantially altered by AI systems rather than captured solely from physical sensors, covering everything from benign mock-ups to deceptive deepfakes.
whyExists: |
  Generation tools made media cheap to invent at scale. Society still treats many media forms as traces of reality, so language is needed for content that looks captured while being composed.
misuse: |
  All synthetic media is collapsed into fraud, or all AI polish is treated as harmless. The category includes product demos, accessibility tools, entertainment, and scams. The risk lives in disclosure, intent, distribution, and harm — not in the mere fact of generation.
