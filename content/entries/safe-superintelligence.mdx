plainDefinition: |
  "Safe Superintelligence" refers both to a specific frontier AI company and to a broader posture in which a lab claims safety is the primary organising principle for pursuing extremely capable systems.
whyExists: |
  The phrase exists because investors and researchers still want frontier upside, while the public increasingly wants some sign that raw capability alone is no longer the entire mission statement.
misuse: |
  It is misused when the name itself is treated as evidence that the governance problem has been solved. A safety-oriented posture may matter. Branding the aspiration does not remove the need for scrutiny, incentives, or operational proof.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means a highly capitalised lab with elite hires, strategic secrecy, minimal product chatter, and a promise that capability and caution can coexist without one eating the other.
