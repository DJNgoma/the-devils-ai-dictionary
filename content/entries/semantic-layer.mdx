plainDefinition: |
  A semantic layer is a shared business-meaning layer that maps data fields, metrics, entities, definitions, and relationships into terms that analytics tools, applications, or AI systems can use consistently.
whyExists: |
  Raw tables rarely speak the language of the business. A semantic layer can reduce duplicated metric definitions, make retrieval more precise, and help AI systems answer with organisational meaning rather than database archaeology.
misuse: |
  It is oversold when a vendor label is treated as if it has fixed the data beneath it. A semantic layer can expose disagreement about customers, revenue, churn, and ownership; it cannot settle those fights by naming them elegantly.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means agreed metric definitions, entity mappings, permissions, lineage, stewardship, versioning, and a process for deciding what changes when the business discovers that "active user" has become a philosophical condition.

  "The enterprise assistant needed a semantic layer because three departments had trained it to calculate revenue with three separate consciences."
