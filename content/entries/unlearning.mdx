  The promise that a model can forget one thing without anyone asking what else it must misremember along the way.
plainDefinition: |
  Unlearning refers to techniques aimed at removing or reducing the influence of particular data, examples, or behaviours from a model after it has already been built.
whyExists: |
  Because privacy requests, legal obligations, harmful content, and contaminated data do not politely arrive before the model is built. Organisations want ways to retract influence without starting from zero.
misuse: |
  By marketing unlearning as exact surgical erasure. In reality it is often approximate, hard to verify, and bound up with trade-offs in performance, evidence, and what counts as having truly forgotten.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means expensive technical work plus an even more expensive verification question: how do you prove a system no longer knows something it was once shaped by?
example: |
  "They wanted unlearning to behave like deleting a file. The model, regrettably, had learned in the more inconvenient way."
