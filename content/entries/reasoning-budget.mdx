devilDefinition: |
  The amount of thought a model is allowed before finance, latency, or policy asks it to develop conviction.
plainDefinition: |
  A reasoning budget is a limit on the computation, tokens, time, cost, or policy allowance a model may spend on deliberation before producing an answer or action.
whyExists: |
  More reasoning can improve some tasks, but it costs money, latency, energy, and sometimes user patience. Systems need controls that decide when deeper reasoning is worth the trade-off.
misuse: |
  It gets treated as a quality dial that only turns upward. More deliberation can still reason from bad premises, overfit a neat plan, or spend handsomely to reach the wrong conclusion.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means task routing, cost controls, latency targets, confidence thresholds, escalation rules, and a clear idea of which work deserves slower, more expensive thought.
example: |
  "The refund bot got a higher reasoning budget for disputed cases and a lower one for checking whether Tuesday comes after Monday."
