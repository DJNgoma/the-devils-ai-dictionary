  An inference provider runs and serves AI models through APIs or managed infrastructure so customers can send inputs and receive outputs without operating the full model stack themselves.

  Because reliable model serving requires GPUs, batching, scaling, observability, routing, and a tolerance for operational pain that many product teams would rather outsource.

  By blurring the line between building a model and hosting one, or by describing a rented endpoint as proprietary intelligence simply because the invoice arrives under a different logo.

  In practice the choice of inference provider shapes latency, price, regional availability, data handling, model access, and how much of your application's reliability now depends on somebody else's serving layer.
