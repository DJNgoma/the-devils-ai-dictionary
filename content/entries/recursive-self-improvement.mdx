devilDefinition: |
  The research programme in which the tool updates the toolmaker and eventually edits the org chart.
plainDefinition: |
  Recursive self-improvement is the proposed capability for an AI system to design and develop a more capable successor, which can then repeat the process. It is stronger than using AI to assist human researchers, automate experiments, or improve a fixed system under human direction.
whyExists: |
  AI systems already contribute to coding, evaluation, data work, and research. If enough of the development cycle became autonomous, improvements could shorten the cycle that produces further improvements, with unusually large consequences for capability forecasting and control.
misuse: |
  Any model that helps train another model is liable to be called recursively self-improving. That skips the central threshold: autonomous successor development and a repeatable capability gain. Anthropic's July 2026 account explicitly says that full recursive self-improvement has not been reached and is not inevitable.
practicalMeaning: |
  Ask which parts of research and development are genuinely autonomous, whether the system can produce and validate a more capable successor, what resources and human approvals remain binding, and which safeguards survive when the developer is also part of the loop.
