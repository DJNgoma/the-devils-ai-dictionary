devilDefinition: |
  A beautifully marketed warning that futuristic hardware demos and durable product-market fit are not the same thing.
plainDefinition: |
  Humane was the startup behind the AI Pin, a wearable AI device that aimed to replace or reduce dependence on the smartphone. The product drew major attention, but Humane later wound down the consumer AI Pin service, and HP announced in February 2025 that it was acquiring key Humane AI capabilities, including the Cosmos platform, talent, and intellectual property.
whyExists: |
  Because Humane became shorthand for both the ambition and the fragility of early AI-device optimism.
misuse: |
  By treating Humane as proof that all AI hardware is doomed, rather than evidence that weak utility, awkward execution, pricing friction, and overhype can kill even well-funded products.
practicalMeaning: |
  A case study in what happens when storytelling outruns product usefulness. Also a reminder that hardware does not get to be vague for long. Consumers carry the thing, charge the thing, and notice when the thing does not justify itself.

Humane deserves precision because it is too easy to turn into a morality play. It did not prove that AI devices are impossible. It proved that theatrical ambition does not excuse weak everyday utility.
