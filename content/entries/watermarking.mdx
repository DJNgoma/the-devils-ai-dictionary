devilDefinition: |
  Invisible ink for model outputs that detectors argue about while open models shrug and continue unmarked.
plainDefinition: |
  Watermarking embeds statistical or cryptographic signals into generated text or media so downstream detectors can estimate whether content came from a particular model or system.
whyExists: |
  Societies want provenance and bot-detection tools. Watermarks promise machine-checkable hints without obvious user-visible marks.
misuse: |
  Treated as unforgeable proof or universal. Robustness against paraphrase, open-weight models, and detector false positives remain contested.
practicalMeaning: |
  Use watermarks as one signal among many. Design policies for false positives and for content that cannot be watermarked.

  "Watermarking caught the bot comments and three humans who write like bots."
