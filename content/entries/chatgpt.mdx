  The product name most likely to mean a chatbot, a model, a coding agent, an office suite, and the company's entire strategy in the same sentence.
plainDefinition: |
  ChatGPT is OpenAI's end-user product across web, desktop, and mobile surfaces. It combines conversational use with models, tools, connected apps, memory, Work mode, artefact creation, and the Codex coding experience; it is not itself one model or one execution mode.
whyExists: |
  Users need one product surface for asking questions, creating deliverables, working across applications, and delegating longer tasks. Bringing Codex into the ChatGPT desktop app also gives coding workflows access to the broader product shell without making Codex and ChatGPT the same technical noun.
misuse: |
  People say "ChatGPT can do X" without naming the model, mode, tool, plugin, entitlement, or surface involved. The merged desktop branding makes this worse: ChatGPT is the application, while Codex remains the coding agent and coding workflow inside it and on other developer surfaces.
practicalMeaning: |
  Product comparisons must record the active model, intelligence level, tools, connected sources, permissions, workspace policy, and whether the task ran locally or in a hosted environment. A result from Work mode or Codex cannot be attributed to a bare model without the surrounding system.
