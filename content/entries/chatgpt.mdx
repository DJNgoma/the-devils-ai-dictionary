  ChatGPT is an OpenAI product: a conversational interface and application environment that may expose different models, tools, and features over time. It is not itself a single model name.

  The distinction matters because user impressions formed inside ChatGPT often get incorrectly projected onto other models, APIs, or applications using different prompts, tools, and constraints.

  People say "ChatGPT can do X" when they may actually mean a particular model inside ChatGPT, a tool available in that interface, or a product flow that is not present in their own application. This creates endless false comparisons.

  In practice it usually means the product experience OpenAI offers to end users. If you are building software, the relevant question is often which model, toolset, and policy layer you can reproduce outside that interface.
