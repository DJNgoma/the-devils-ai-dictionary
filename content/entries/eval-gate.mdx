  A release blocker that says the model may not ship until the numbers clear the bar—bureaucracy that sometimes saves you.
plainDefinition: |
  An eval gate is a deployment or merge control that requires automated evaluation metrics to meet predefined thresholds before a model, prompt, or agent change is released.
whyExists: |
  Without gates, quality and safety regressions ship on vibes and schedule pressure. Gates encode non-negotiables into the pipeline.
misuse: |
  Set on vanity metrics, or chronically overridden. A gate that always opens is decoration.
practicalMeaning: |
  Choose metrics tied to user harm and product success, version them, limit overrides with audit logs, and keep suites fast enough to run always.

  "The eval gate blocked a prompt change that doubled toxic completions on a quiet slice."
