  The horizon word used when "better than last quarter" no longer feels expensive enough.
plainDefinition: |
  AGI usually refers to a hypothetical AI system that can perform a wide range of cognitive tasks at or above human level with substantial adaptability across domains.
whyExists: |
  The term exists because narrow systems can be impressive without being general, and people need a name for the stronger claim that a machine could transfer competence broadly rather than merely excel on selected tasks.
misuse: |
  By treating it as a launch category, a press strategy, or a vague aura that descends on any model after a particularly strong demo. AGI is a very large claim, not a mood.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice the term often functions as strategic theatre: it shapes fundraising, policy lobbying, hiring plans, and media attention long before anyone agrees on what threshold would actually count.
example: |
  "The deck mentioned AGI on slide three, which saved everyone the trouble of asking how the product planned to make money this year."

AGI remains useful as a provocation and dangerous as a schedule. Once it enters a room, timelines harden, valuations inflate, and ordinary questions about reliability start dressing more modestly.
