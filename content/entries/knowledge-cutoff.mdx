plainDefinition: |
  A knowledge cutoff is the latest point in time reflected in a model's internal knowledge without fresh retrieval or live access to newer information.
whyExists: |
  Models are built from data gathered during a finite period. However broad that period was, it still ends somewhere, which means the model's default knowledge eventually becomes stale.
misuse: |
  By treating the cutoff as either a precise magical boundary or as something that no longer matters because the interface looks current. A model can sound up to date long after its internal map stopped being refreshed.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means any question about recent events, changing laws, product releases, prices, or people who change jobs needs fresh sources instead of trusting the model's memory and posture.
