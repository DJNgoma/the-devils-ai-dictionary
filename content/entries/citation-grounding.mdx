devilDefinition: |
  The old-fashioned idea that a citation should support the sentence standing on it, not merely loiter nearby.
plainDefinition: |
  Citation grounding is the practice of ensuring that cited sources directly support the claims they are attached to in an AI-generated answer.
whyExists: |
  AI systems can produce plausible citations, weakly related sources, or citations that support a neighbouring claim but not the actual sentence. Grounding ties claims back to evidence more rigorously.
misuse: |
  It gets reduced to "has citations". A footnote can be decorative, stale, irrelevant, or contradicted by the source it points to.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means checking claim-level support, preserving source passages or metadata, distinguishing direct evidence from background reading, and refusing to let citations become costume jewellery.
example: |
  "The answer had five citations, but citation grounding found that only one supported the revenue claim and the others were standing around looking official."
