  A model card is a documentation artefact describing a model's intended use, training data overview, evaluation results, limitations, and ethical considerations, popularised as a transparency practice.
whyExists: |
  Models shipped without shared context about scope and failure modes. Cards created a standard-ish place to put that context.
misuse: |
  Treated as complete safety proof or marketing brochure. A card can be outdated, selective, or disconnected from the deployed checkpoint.
practicalMeaning: |
  Require cards for production models, version them with checkpoints, and link to live eval dashboards rather than static PDFs alone.
