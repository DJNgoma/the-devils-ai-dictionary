devilDefinition: |
  The awkward distance between what the system could probably do and what the product, policy, or public story is presently willing to admit.
plainDefinition: |
  Capability overhang is the gap between a system's latent capabilities and the narrower behaviour currently exposed through its interface, safeguards, compute limits, prompting, or deployment choices.
whyExists: |
  The term exists because product behaviour is not the same thing as system potential. A model can look ordinary in one wrapper and considerably more capable once the scaffolding, tools, time horizon, or constraints change.
misuse: |
  It gets used as a spooky substitute for evidence. People invoke capability overhang to imply imminent AGI, hidden danger, or secret brilliance without specifying what new affordance, training signal, or operating condition would actually unlock the change.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means asking whether the next jump will come from better tools, longer rollouts, cheaper inference, weaker guardrails, more deliberate prompting, or simply from finally measuring the system properly instead of admiring the demo surface.
example: |
  "The product looked politely limited until tool access and a longer action horizon revealed a capability overhang nobody had budgeted to supervise."

Capability overhang is useful when it keeps a team from confusing today's interface with tomorrow's operating problem. It is less useful when it becomes a haunted-house synonym for "something dramatic might happen eventually."
