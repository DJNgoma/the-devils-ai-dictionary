  A national AI-for-science programme whose first experiment is coordinating everyone who has volunteered to coordinate it.
plainDefinition: |
  Genesis Mission is a United States Department of Energy initiative intended to connect the country's 17 national laboratories, scientific facilities, datasets, advanced computing, AI systems, and private partners into a coordinated platform for AI-assisted science.
whyExists: |
  Scientific discovery is constrained by fragmented data, scarce computing, specialised instruments, and the work required to move between simulation and experiment. A national programme can coordinate access, engineering, security, and research priorities across institutions.
misuse: |
  Partner commitments, cloud credits, programme offices, and ambitious targets are reported as one already unified research system. A mission can align budgets and participants before it proves interoperability, researcher access, reproducibility, or faster discovery.
practicalMeaning: |
  Track funded projects, access rules, data agreements, compute delivery, laboratory integration, security boundaries, milestones, publications, and the ownership of resulting intellectual property. Vendor contributions support the mission; they do not define its public outcomes.
