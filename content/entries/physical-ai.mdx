plainDefinition: |
  "Physical AI" is a broad industry label for applying modern AI systems to embodied machines such as robots, vehicles, and industrial equipment operating in real environments.
whyExists: |
  The term exists because language and image models do not move boxes, navigate warehouses, or survive factory floors by themselves. Embodied systems need perception, control, planning, simulation, and safety engineering tied to physical constraints.
misuse: |
  It is misused whenever a polished robot demo is treated as evidence that deployment is near. Hardware, safety, reliability, maintenance, and integration remain stubbornly unimpressed by keynote vocabulary.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means expensive sensor stacks, simulation pipelines, teleoperation fallbacks, long deployment cycles, and a great deal of engineering dedicated to the fact that the physical world does not accept almost right.
