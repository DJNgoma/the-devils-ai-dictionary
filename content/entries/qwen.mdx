  Qwen is a family of models associated with Alibaba Cloud and related research and product efforts. It can refer to multiple models and variants rather than a single unified system.
whyExists: |
  The term matters because discussions of AI often collapse into a very small US-centric vendor set. Qwen is one of the model families that reminds people the landscape is broader, more competitive, and more operationally varied than the headlines imply.
misuse: |
  It gets used as if "Qwen" alone were enough information. In reality, capability, licensing, deployment fit, and tool support depend on the specific model and version. Saying "we benchmarked Qwen" is only the beginning of the sentence.

  In practice it usually means evaluating one of several model variants for cost, quality, deployment flexibility, coding, agent workflows, multimodal use, or localisation needs. The right comparison is not with a brand in general, but with a task and a constraint set.
