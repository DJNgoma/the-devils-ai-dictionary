plainDefinition: |
  A confused deputy is a program with authority that is misled into exercising that authority on behalf of an attacker; in AI systems this often involves tools, agents, or connectors with broad credentials.
whyExists: |
  Agents with tools are deputies. Prompt injection and hostile content try to confuse them into misusing privileges.
misuse: |
  Mentioned only as trivia. It is a design threat model for every high-privilege integration.
practicalMeaning: |
  Minimise authority, bind actions to user intent with confirmations, separate trusts per tool, and never let untrusted content freely choose privileged operations.
