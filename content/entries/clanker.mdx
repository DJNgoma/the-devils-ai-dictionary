  "Clanker" is a derogatory slang term for robots and AI systems, especially chatbots or automations that feel intrusive, fake-human, or threatening to human work.
whyExists: |
  Because people wanted a short insult for the new class of machines now appearing in customer support, content feeds, creative tools, and workplace software. It gives irritation, labour anxiety, and anti-bot sentiment one noisy noun.
misuse: |
  By using it as if all automation is the same, or by pretending the joke is harmless when it is clearly borrowing the theatre and cadence of real slurs. It is most useful as a signal of resentment toward a technology category, not as an excuse to rehearse bigotry with metallic props.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means "I am talking about an AI system I find annoying, dehumanising, low-quality, job-threatening, or impossible to avoid", whether that system is a chatbot, a voice menu, a content engine, or a delivery robot.

What matters about clanker is not whether the machine has feelings. It is that the insult reveals a public mood: people increasingly experience AI not as abstract progress but as an unwanted substitute for service, attention, and paid work.
