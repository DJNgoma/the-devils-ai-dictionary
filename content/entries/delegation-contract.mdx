devilDefinition: |
  The part of delegation where someone finally writes down what "handle this" was supposed to mean.
plainDefinition: |
  A delegation contract describes the scope, authority, inputs, constraints, expected output, and completion criteria for work delegated to another agent, tool, team, or person.
whyExists: |
  Delegation is useful only when the receiving party knows what it may decide, what it must not touch, what evidence it should return, and how success will be judged. In agent systems, those boundaries need to be explicit because politeness is a poor access-control model.
misuse: |
  It gets replaced by hopeful task descriptions. "Research this", "fix the bug", or "take care of onboarding" may be enough for a colleague with context. For an agent or subagent, it often means the missing assumptions become the implementation.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means bounded write scope, required context, allowed tools, decision rights, output format, stopping conditions, review expectations, and a record of who remains accountable for the delegated work.
