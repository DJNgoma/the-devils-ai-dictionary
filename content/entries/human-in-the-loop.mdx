  A workflow design in which a person reviews, approves, corrects, or intervenes in an automated system's output before or during action.

  Because some decisions remain too costly, risky, or ambiguous to automate blindly. Human review is how organisations keep accountability attached to a named person rather than a press release.

  By invoking it as a magic safety phrase when the human is undertrained, overloaded, or expected to rubber-stamp machine output at impossible speed.

  In practice it usually means queues, review fatigue, awkward staffing maths, and the discovery that "just have a human check it" is not a design but a labour budget.
