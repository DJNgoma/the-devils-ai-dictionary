  A clue about where content came from, not a courtroom transcript with better metadata.
plainDefinition: |
  A provenance signal is any marker, metadata, watermark, credential, signature, or contextual clue that helps indicate the origin, history, or authenticity of content.
whyExists: |
  Synthetic media, edited content, reposts, and automated generation make origin harder to judge. Provenance signals help systems and people assess where content came from and whether it has been altered.
misuse: |
  They get mistaken for proof. A signal can be missing, stripped, forged, misread, or detached from the thing it was meant to describe.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means combining metadata, platform context, cryptographic checks, editorial judgement, and scepticism rather than outsourcing trust to one badge.

  "The provenance signal suggested the image had edit history, which was useful until the platform treated that suggestion as divine testimony."
