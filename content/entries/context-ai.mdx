devilDefinition: |
  A platform for giving agents more of your organisation's actual context, and therefore more of your organisation's actual permissions.
plainDefinition: |
  Context.ai is an AI workspace and agent platform built around connecting company systems, capturing workflow context, and running agents against real tasks. Its current pitch emphasises enterprise deployments, connectors, sandboxes, and controlled execution surfaces rather than a generic chat interface.
whyExists: |
  Because generic models do not know your firm's documents, tools, processes, or standards. Products like Context.ai exist to make agents operate against company-specific context instead of improvising from a prompt and a prayer.
misuse: |
  By talking about "context" as if adding more integrations automatically produces better judgement. More context can improve usefulness. It can also widen the blast radius when tokens, connectors, and OAuth grants are handled with the usual startup optimism.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means connectors, workflow templates, sandboxed runs, eval loops, and an enterprise promise that agents can work across your actual systems rather than merely chat about them. It also now means a public lesson in how broad AI-tool permissions can become someone else's incident report.
