plainDefinition: |
  Hosted MCP refers to MCP servers or MCP-compatible integrations run as managed infrastructure by a vendor, platform, or central internal team.
whyExists: |
  Teams do not always want to run every connector themselves. Hosted MCP can reduce setup work, centralise maintenance, and make integrations easier to distribute across clients or organisations.
misuse: |
  It gets sold as simplicity while quietly moving the hard questions into authentication, data residency, logging, tenancy, versioning, and vendor dependency.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means deciding who operates the server, who can inspect its behaviour, where data flows, how permissions are enforced, and what happens when the hosted layer changes.

  "Hosted MCP saved the team from maintaining the connector, then introduced a procurement meeting so the universe could stay balanced."
