  A fabricated customer with infinite availability and the same fatal weakness as every fabricated customer: it does not pay.
plainDefinition: |
  A synthetic user is an AI-generated stand-in for a human user, used to simulate feedback, behaviour, testing, or product research scenarios.
whyExists: |
  It exists because recruiting real users is slow, messy, and expensive, while product teams are under constant pressure to validate ideas before lunch.
misuse: |
  It gets oversold as a replacement for actual user research. Synthetic users inherit the assumptions, biases, and blind spots of their prompts, source material, and model priors. They can imitate a segment. They cannot become one.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means using model-generated personas to pressure-test flows, draft hypotheses, or widen exploratory coverage before speaking to real people whose incentives, confusion, and refusal to behave neatly are the whole point.
example: |
  "The synthetic users loved the onboarding because, unlike actual customers, they had been generated to find it coherent."
