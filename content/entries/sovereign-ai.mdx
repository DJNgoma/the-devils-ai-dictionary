devilDefinition: |
  The promise that a nation can keep its models, compute, data, and strategic dependence inside the border, or at least inside the speech.
plainDefinition: |
  "Sovereign AI" refers to national or regional efforts to build, host, govern, or control AI infrastructure and capabilities domestically rather than depending entirely on foreign providers.
whyExists: |
  It exists because AI capacity now touches economic leverage, public services, security concerns, language coverage, and industrial policy. Governments and large regional blocs do not enjoy discovering that critical capability is rented from somewhere else.
misuse: |
  It is misused when procurement theatre is presented as autonomy, or when a datacentre announcement stands in for a credible ecosystem of talent, governance, hardware supply, and locally useful models. Flags are cheaper than capability.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means public-private funding, domestic hosting requirements, preferred vendor partnerships, regional data controls, and a long negotiation over what degree of dependence still counts as sovereignty.

  "The sovereign AI strategy was unveiled with great ceremony and a familiar dependence on imported chips, imported cloud, and imported certainty."
