  The executive voice of AI incumbency: calm enough to sound inevitable, careful enough to make strategic delay resemble wisdom.
plainDefinition: |
  Sundar Pichai is the CEO of Google and Alphabet. In AI conversations, his name often stands for Google's attempt to convert enormous distribution, infrastructure, and research depth into an orderly response to the generative-AI era.
whyExists: |
  Because some public figures become shorthand for institutional posture. Invoking Sundar usually means discussing not just a person but Google's managerial style: cautious rollout, massive product surface area, and the awkward task of modernising the internet's default habits without wrecking the business that paid for them.
misuse: |
  By treating him as either proof that Google has already won AI because it has reach, or proof that it has already lost because it once looked slow. Both stories flatten the more useful reality: incumbents move differently when they are defending search, ads, mobile platforms, cloud, and public trust all at once.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice, invoking Sundar usually means Google's AI strategy under executive discipline: Gemini folded through consumer and enterprise products, AI Overviews inserted into search, and a company trying to make aggressive technical change look like prudent continuity.
