plainDefinition: |
  Claude Design is Anthropic Labs' conversational product for generating and refining designs, prototypes, slides, one-pagers, and other visual work from prompts, files, and codebases.
whyExists: |
  The idea-to-mockup gap is still slow, expensive, and socially awkward for people without design training. Claude Design exists to turn rough intent into something visual fast enough that the meeting can continue pretending it was always headed somewhere.
misuse: |
  It gets sold as if design has been reduced to prompt fluency plus a brand kit. Fast first drafts are real. So are hierarchy, judgement, interaction sense, and the quiet labour of making a design feel intentional rather than merely complete.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means quick prototype generation, design-system ingestion, export to formats normal companies still use, and a cleaner handoff path into tools like Claude Code or Canva when the rough idea has to become a real asset.
