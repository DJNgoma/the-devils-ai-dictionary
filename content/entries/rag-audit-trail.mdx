  The receipt for the answer, requested shortly after the answer has become expensive.
plainDefinition: |
  A RAG audit trail is the record of which query, sources, chunks, rankings, filters, citations, model inputs, and answer outputs were used when a retrieval-augmented system produced a response.
whyExists: |
  Once RAG answers influence support, legal, compliance, medical, financial, or operational decisions, teams need to know what evidence was retrieved, what was excluded, and how the final answer was grounded.
misuse: |
  It is confused with showing citations in the user interface. Citations are useful, but an audit trail also needs retriever state, permissions, versions, timestamps, filters, model inputs, and enough detail to reproduce or investigate the answer.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means logging retrieval requests, source identities, chunk IDs, ranking scores, permission filters, prompt context, generated claims, cited sources, blocked sources, model versions, and retention rules that will survive contact with compliance.

  "The RAG audit trail showed that the assistant cited the policy correctly and ignored the newer exception nobody had indexed."
