  Responsible scaling refers to policies—often lab-authored Responsible Scaling Policies (RSPs)—that condition training or deployment of more capable models on safety evaluations, mitigations, and governance gates.
whyExists: |
  Capability jumps raised catastrophic-risk concerns. Labs needed frameworks that link scale decisions to measured risk tiers.

  Treated as proof of safety, or as pure PR. Policies vary, enforcement is internal, and thresholds can move under competition.
practicalMeaning: |
  Read the actual commitments, eval methods, and pause conditions. Ask what is independently auditable versus self-reported.
