plainDefinition: |
  Model routing is the practice of directing each request to a chosen model or endpoint based on rules, classifiers, or policies—typically to balance cost, latency, capability, and risk.
whyExists: |
  One model is rarely optimal for every query. Systems needed a control plane for 'good enough versus best available' without rewriting the product for each call.
misuse: |
  Presented as free optimisation. Bad routers misclassify hard tasks onto weak models, or thrash between endpoints until latency and bills both rise.
practicalMeaning: |
  Define routing features (length, tool need, risk tier), log decisions, measure quality per route, and keep a safe fallback. Treat the router as production logic with tests.
