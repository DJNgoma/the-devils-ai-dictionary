devilDefinition: |
  An adjective for products born after the hype wave hit, and therefore convinced they should inherit its authority.
plainDefinition: |
  "AI-native" describes a product or company built with AI systems as a core assumption from the beginning, rather than adding them later as a feature layer.
whyExists: |
  The term exists because retrofitting AI into a legacy product often creates awkward workflows, pricing, architecture, and expectations. Newer companies want a way to signal that the model is not an afterthought.
misuse: |
  It gets abused whenever a chatbot bolted onto a conventional product is treated as proof of a new species. Sometimes "AI-native" means a genuinely different operating model. Sometimes it means the landing page learned a new adjective.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means product design, support costs, staffing, onboarding, and margins all assume model behaviour will sit in the critical path. That can create leverage. It can also create a business whose unit economics have an unusually intimate relationship with latency and inference bills.
example: |
