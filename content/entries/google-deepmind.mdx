plainDefinition: |
  Google DeepMind is Google's central AI research and frontier-model organisation. It combines the original DeepMind organisation with key Google AI research efforts, and sits behind research programmes and products including Gemini, AlphaFold, robotics work, safety research, and broader frontier-model development.
whyExists: |
  Because people still say "DeepMind" as if it were a free-floating research monastery. It is now a major unit inside Google, with both scientific prestige and the platform power that comes from sitting inside one of the largest technology companies on earth.
misuse: |
  By treating Google DeepMind as either a pure research institution or merely another product division. It is both more interesting and more awkward than that: a frontier lab nested inside a company that wants research breakthroughs to become products at scale.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means Google's long-horizon AI engine: the part of the company responsible for major research advances, frontier models, safety work, and many of the technical foundations that later surface through Gemini and other Google products.
