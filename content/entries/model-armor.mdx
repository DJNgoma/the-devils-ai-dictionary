  Model Armor is Google Cloud's product surface for adding prompt, response, and content protection controls around AI applications.
whyExists: |
  Organisations deploying AI systems need ways to inspect prompts and outputs for policy violations, unsafe content, prompt injection risks, and other application-level safety concerns.
misuse: |
  It gets mistaken for universal protection. A safety product can reduce some classes of risk, but it does not remove the need for permissions, architecture, logging, testing, and common sense.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means adding a managed safety layer to AI traffic, then deciding which policies apply, what gets blocked, how false positives are handled, and who reviews the exceptions.
