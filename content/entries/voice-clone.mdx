plainDefinition: |
  A voice clone is a synthetic reproduction of a person's voice, generated from recorded samples or learned speaker characteristics so that new speech sounds like that person.
whyExists: |
  It exists because synthetic speech can support dubbing, accessibility, localisation, narration, call automation, and personalisation. The same capability also makes impersonation cheaper and consent harder to treat as an afterthought.
misuse: |
  It is misused when treated as a party trick instead of an identity, fraud, labour, and consent problem. The quality of the audio is only part of the issue; the harder question is who was allowed to make the voice speak, for whom, and for how long.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means speaker consent, sample provenance, usage limits, watermarking or disclosure, revocation rules, fraud controls, call-centre policies, and contracts that say whether a voice may be reused, trained on, or sold onward.
