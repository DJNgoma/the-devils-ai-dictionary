plainDefinition: |
  A synthetic content label is a notice or marker indicating that media, text, audio, video, or another artifact was generated or materially altered by AI.
whyExists: |
  People need context when evaluating content, especially in politics, news, identity, evidence, advertising, and creative work. Labels can support disclosure and reduce some forms of deception.
misuse: |
  Labels get treated as literacy. A label can be missed, removed, vague, disputed, or misunderstood, and it does not explain whether the content is harmful, truthful, satirical, or benign.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means platforms and products need disclosure rules, enforcement, provenance support, user education, and a way to handle mixed human and AI editing.
