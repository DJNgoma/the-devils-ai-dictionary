plainDefinition: |
  Deepfake consent is the permission, scope, evidence, and revocation framework around creating or distributing synthetic media that uses a person's face, voice, likeness, performance, or identity.
whyExists: |
  It exists because synthetic media can imitate real people at commercial scale. Legitimate uses need a way to prove who agreed, what they agreed to, where it may appear, how long it lasts, and what happens when permission is withdrawn.
misuse: |
  It is misused when a vague release form, platform checkbox, or old media contract is treated as permanent permission for every future synthetic use. Consent is not very meaningful if the person cannot understand, limit, audit, or revoke the thing being done in their name.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means signed scope, identity verification, asset provenance, compensation terms, permitted channels, territory, duration, training restrictions, revocation handling, and records that survive beyond the campaign deck.
