devilDefinition: |
  The assistant that would like to helpfully hover over every tab, meeting, and message until convenience and continuous surveillance become a branding distinction.
plainDefinition: |
  An ambient agent is an AI assistant designed to stay persistently available across apps or devices, using ongoing context to offer suggestions, trigger actions, or surface help without waiting for a fresh prompt each time.
whyExists: |
  Because users dislike restarting context in every app, while vendors would prefer to become the default layer sitting above email, documents, meetings, browsers, and operating systems.
misuse: |
  By treating constant presence as intelligence. An assistant can be ambient simply by being everywhere, collecting context, and interrupting with confidence. That does not mean it understands priorities, privacy, or timing.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means more permissions, more background context capture, more cross-app hooks, and a delicate trade between convenience, distraction, and the quiet feeling that your software now has opinions about your calendar.
example: |
  "The ambient agent was excellent at finding follow-up tasks and even better at reminding everyone how much data it could see."
