  A national strategy rendered in megawatts, accelerators, and the comforting grammar of sovereignty.
plainDefinition: |
  National AI infrastructure is a coordinated, country-scale foundation of compute, networking, data, models, facilities, institutions, and access rules intended to support domestic AI research and industry. It may be government funded, privately operated, or built through a consortium.
whyExists: |
  Advanced AI requires capital, energy, specialist hardware, data, and operating expertise that few organisations can assemble alone. National programmes can pool those resources and steer access towards research, public services, or strategic industries.
misuse: |
  One large vendor-backed data centre is declared national infrastructure, then national infrastructure is declared sovereignty. Domestic location does not establish domestic control, broad access, local capability, resilience, or public benefit.
practicalMeaning: |
  Inspect ownership, funding, capacity, energy, procurement, model and data policy, eligibility, pricing, governance, skills development, and delivery dates. Japan's planned Noetra facility is a current example; NVIDIA's description of it as the world's first remains a vendor claim.
