  Process supervision provides feedback on intermediate reasoning steps or actions—via humans or models—rather than supervising only final outcomes.

  Outcome-only labels are sparse and allow lucky correctness. Process labels teach better trajectories on hard multi-step tasks.

  Use process supervision where steps are meaningful and checkable; combine with outcome metrics; watch for reward hacking of the process judge.
