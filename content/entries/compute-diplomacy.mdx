plainDefinition: |
  Compute diplomacy is the negotiation of AI compute access through national policy, chip export controls, data-centre investment, cloud regions, strategic partnerships, subsidies, and cross-border infrastructure deals.
whyExists: |
  It exists because AI capacity has become economic leverage and statecraft. Governments, vendors, and investors now treat chips, cloud regions, energy supply, and local hosting as instruments of influence as well as infrastructure.
misuse: |
  It is misused when a diplomatic announcement is presented as actual capability. A memorandum, investment pledge, or preferred-vendor relationship may improve access, but it can also deepen dependence while giving everyone a better podium.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means asking who gets the chips, who owns the facility, which laws apply, which export controls bite, who trains the workforce, where the profits flow, and what happens when the partnership becomes politically inconvenient.
