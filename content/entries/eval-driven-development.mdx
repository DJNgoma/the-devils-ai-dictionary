devilDefinition: |
  The radical idea that if your product depends on stochastic behaviour, you should probably measure something before calling the demo a platform.
plainDefinition: |
  Eval-driven development is the practice of using targeted evaluations to guide prompt changes, model selection, tool design, and release decisions for AI systems.
whyExists: |
  Traditional software tests do not fully capture AI behaviour, while pure vibes scale poorly once money, customers, or reputations are involved. Teams need a repeatable way to detect regressions and compare alternatives.
misuse: |
  It is misused when a handful of flattering examples are promoted to an evaluation strategy, or when teams treat proxy metrics as if they were the product itself. An eval can discipline judgement. It cannot replace it.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means curated test sets, grading rubrics, automated checks, and before-after comparisons tied to real tasks. The mature version influences release gates. The immature version produces a dashboard nobody trusts but everyone screenshots.
example: |
  "Eval-driven development arrived the day they stopped arguing about prompts in Slack and started arguing about failure rates instead."
