  The list every governance programme asks for after discovering the organisation has been shipping intelligence through spreadsheets, vendor portals, and brave endpoints with no forwarding address.
plainDefinition: |
  A model inventory is a maintained catalogue of models, AI systems, wrappers, endpoints, vendors, owners, versions, uses, data dependencies, risk tiers, and deployment status.
whyExists: |
  It exists because an organisation cannot govern, monitor, retire, insure, or defend systems it cannot name. As AI spreads through products and departments, the inventory becomes the map before the controls can be more than theatre.
misuse: |
  It gets reduced to a spreadsheet made once for an audit, or narrowed to only the models built by the central AI team. The inconvenient work is finding the bought tools, embedded features, shadow pilots, spreadsheet miracles, and vendor models already touching business decisions.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means ownership records, risk classification, approved uses, data lineage, model versions, vendor contracts, deployment locations, monitoring status, incident history, and a process for updating the record when the system changes.
