  An AI shopping agent is an assistant that can search for products, compare options, apply constraints, suggest substitutions, track availability, and sometimes reserve or purchase items for a person or organisation.

  It exists because shopping is partly search, partly decision support, and partly administrative nuisance. Agents promise to handle the comparison work, but the moment money can move, helpfulness becomes a control problem.

  It gets sold as effortless convenience while underplaying persuasion, affiliate incentives, substitution logic, data sharing, returns, and buyer remorse. A shopping agent is not neutral just because it speaks in the first person.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means preference capture, merchant access, budget envelopes, approval thresholds, payment permissions, refund paths, audit trails, and a clear point where the agent must stop and ask.
