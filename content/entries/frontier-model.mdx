devilDefinition: |
  A model said to be at the frontier, which often means "very capable, very expensive, and deeply useful to mention in a fundraising narrative."
plainDefinition: |
  A frontier model is generally understood to mean one of the most capable and resource-intensive models available at a given time, usually near the top end of performance on a range of tasks. It is not a sharply defined scientific category.
whyExists: |
  The term exists because the field needed a way to talk about the leading edge of capability without listing every model individually. It can be useful shorthand when discussing high-end systems, their risks, and their costs.
misuse: |
  It is often treated as a formal badge rather than a loose ranking label. Vendors, commentators, and investors may use it to imply inevitability, superiority, or strategic importance beyond what the actual use case warrants.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means a large, expensive model family with broad capability, strong benchmarks, and significant operational or policy attention. For many products, the question is not whether a model is frontier. The question is whether it is necessary.
