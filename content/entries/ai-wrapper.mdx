  A startup whose signature innovation is adding branding, billing, and investor adjectives to somebody else's capability.
plainDefinition: |
  A product or company built mainly by packaging existing model APIs with a narrower interface, workflow, or market focus.
whyExists: |
  Because base models are broad, raw, and inconveniently generic. Someone still has to choose a use case, shape the workflow, handle integration, and sell the result to people who do not want to assemble it themselves.
misuse: |
  By using "wrapper" as a complete dismissal. Some wrappers are flimsy tollbooths. Others are the only part customers actually need. The insult often tells you more about the speaker's ego than the product's margin.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means a company racing to build distribution, workflow lock-in, and a reason to exist before the underlying capability becomes a menu item elsewhere.
