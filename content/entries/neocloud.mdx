  A cloud provider whose most disruptive feature is often discovering that scarce Nvidia hardware can be resold with excellent posture.
plainDefinition: |
  "Neocloud" is an informal label for newer cloud providers focused on AI infrastructure, especially GPU capacity for training and inference, rather than broad general-purpose enterprise cloud offerings.
whyExists: |
  The term exists because demand for accelerators outran easy supply, while many teams wanted alternatives to the hyperscalers on price, access, support, geography, or simple willingness to answer the phone.
misuse: |
  It is misused whenever specialised capacity is treated as a permanent moat. Owning or brokering GPUs can be valuable. It does not automatically solve networking, reliability, data governance, procurement, or the small inconvenience of debt.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means a provider that can get you machines faster, sometimes cheaper, sometimes with better human support, in exchange for narrower platform breadth and a closer relationship with the physics of hardware finance.
