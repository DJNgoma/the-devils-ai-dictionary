devilDefinition: |
  The part of the model's inner monologue vendors alternately market as proof of thought and hide as a trade secret.
plainDefinition: |
  A reasoning trace is the sequence of intermediate deliberative steps a model produces while solving a task, whether shown directly, summarised for the user, or kept internal by the provider.
whyExists: |
  It exists because some reasoning-oriented systems allocate extra compute to intermediate steps before producing a final answer, and those steps can improve performance on harder tasks.
misuse: |
  The term is abused whenever a long answer is treated as evidence of deep reasoning, or when a visible summary is mistaken for the full internal process. What you see may be curated explanation rather than raw cognition.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means more latency, more tokens, higher cost, better performance on some tasks, and a new set of arguments about transparency, gaming, privacy, and whether exposing traces helps users or merely helps them feel impressed.

  "The product demo featured a reasoning trace, which was excellent theatre and only partial evidence."
