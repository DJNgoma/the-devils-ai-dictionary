  The dedicated silicon every device launch now invokes to prove the future has arrived locally, efficiently, and preferably in this quarter's hardware cycle.
plainDefinition: |
  An NPU is a specialised processor designed to accelerate machine-learning workloads, especially on-device inference tasks such as speech, vision, transcription, or smaller language-model features.
whyExists: |
  Because CPUs and GPUs are not always the best fit for constant local inference, and device makers want better performance per watt for AI features that cannot afford cloud latency or cloud disclosure.
misuse: |
  By treating the presence of an NPU as evidence that meaningful AI features exist, or by presenting the acronym as a product benefit without saying what models fit, what latency improves, or what the software stack can actually use.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice an NPU determines which features can run on-device, how responsive and private they can be, and whether "AI PC" or "AI phone" means a new capability or merely a fresh sticker on the box.
