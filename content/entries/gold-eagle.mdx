devilDefinition: |
  A federal clearinghouse for the awkward interval between an AI finding a vulnerability and everyone agreeing who must fix it.
plainDefinition: |
  Gold Eagle is a United States government initiative for coordinating the validation, disclosure, and remediation of software vulnerabilities discovered at AI-accelerated scale, with particular concern for critical infrastructure and widely used systems.
whyExists: |
  AI security tools can produce vulnerability reports faster than vendors, researchers, and government programmes can validate, route, prioritise, and patch them. A clearinghouse aims to prevent genuine findings from becoming either an unmanaged disclosure queue or an attacker shopping list.
misuse: |
  Coordination is presented as automatic remediation. A clearinghouse cannot make noisy findings valid, compel every maintainer to patch, eliminate disclosure disputes, or ensure that fixes reach exposed systems before exploit details spread.
practicalMeaning: |
  Researchers and AI developers need submission rules, evidence, severity triage, confidentiality, vendor contact, remediation deadlines, and escalation paths. Operators should ask how the initiative handles duplicate findings, false positives, unsupported software, and cross-border dependencies.
example: |
