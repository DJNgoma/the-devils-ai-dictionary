devilDefinition: |
  AI running close enough to the user that the cloud bill disappears from view and the hardware bill becomes a personal matter.
plainDefinition: |
  Local AI means running some or all model inference, retrieval, storage, or agent tooling on hardware controlled by the user or organisation rather than sending every operation to a third-party hosted service. It ranges from a model on one laptop to a private on-premises cluster.
whyExists: |
  Local execution can improve privacy, latency, offline availability, customisation, cost predictability, and control over models and data. It also lets products work where connectivity, data residency, or external-service dependence is unacceptable.
misuse: |
  "Local" is treated as a complete security and privacy verdict. A local app may still download models, check for updates, call remote tools, send telemetry, expose a network server, or process data through plugins whose boundaries the interface neglects to mention.
practicalMeaning: |
  Teams must specify what runs locally: model inference, embeddings, document parsing, memory, tools, logs, and storage may each live somewhere different. They also inherit model updates, hardware capacity, patching, access control, backups, energy use, and evaluation.
example: |
  "The assistant was local AI because inference stayed on the laptop; the web-search tool, licence server, and crash reporter had made other arrangements."
