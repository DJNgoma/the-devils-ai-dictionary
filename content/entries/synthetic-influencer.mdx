plainDefinition: |
  A synthetic influencer is a fictional or AI-generated persona used to promote products, publish content, attract followers, or represent a brand as if it were a social media personality.
whyExists: |
  It exists because brands want repeatable personalities that can be localised, scheduled, licensed, and revised without negotiating with a human being who has opinions, agents, fatigue, or old tweets.
misuse: |
  It gets abused when a designed persona is treated as authentic community presence. A synthetic influencer can perform a style and hold an audience, but it cannot honestly live a life, disclose a private experience, or provide lived endorsement.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means brand governance, disclosure rules, likeness rights, synthetic content labelling, moderation, audience trust, and a careful answer to who is responsible when the invented person says something expensive.
