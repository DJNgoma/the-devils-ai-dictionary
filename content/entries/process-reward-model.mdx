plainDefinition: |
  A process reward model (PRM) scores intermediate reasoning steps or partial solutions, providing denser training or search signal than outcome-only rewards that judge only the final answer.
whyExists: |
  Hard tasks have long traces. Waiting until the end to say 'wrong' wastes signal and encourages lucky final guesses after messy reasoning.
misuse: |
  Treated as objective truth about reasoning. PRMs can be gamed, miscalibrated, or biased toward verbose-looking steps.
practicalMeaning: |
  Use PRMs in search, RL, or critique loops with careful step labels and held-out process evals. Monitor for reward hacking that invents plausible junk steps.
