plainDefinition: |
  A slop firewall is a defensive system that blocks, rate-limits, demotes, or quarantines low-value AI-generated content before it reaches users, search indexes, marketplaces, support queues, or knowledge bases.
whyExists: |
  It exists because filtering after exposure is often too late. Once synthetic filler enters a marketplace, inbox, or search corpus, the cost shifts to readers, reviewers, ranking systems, and every legitimate contributor now forced to prove they are not debris.
misuse: |
  It gets used as security theatre when teams claim protection without defining the blocked surface, review policy, or tolerance for false positives. A firewall with no enforcement plan is just a dashboard wearing boots.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means input controls, reputation signals, rate limits, provenance checks, source allowlists, automated triage, human escalation, and a clear distinction between low-quality generation and legitimate AI-assisted work.
