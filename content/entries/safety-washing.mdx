devilDefinition: |
  The art of polishing the safety language until it shines brightly enough to distract from the release incentives underneath.
plainDefinition: |
  Safety washing is the practice of using the language, imagery, or rituals of AI safety without meaningfully changing release discipline, incentives, risk controls, or accountability.
whyExists: |
  Because companies, labs, and governments all benefit from looking responsible, and safety vocabulary provides a respectable public dialect for discussing systems that still need to ship, sell, and win headlines.
misuse: |
  By labelling every imperfect safety effort as fraudulent. Not every incomplete control is washing. The phrase is most useful when the safety story is visibly performative and cannot materially slow, shape, or stop the risky thing.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means glossy policy prose, selective mention of evaluations, carefully staged red-team references, and very little evidence that safety work has authority over product timelines or commercial pressure.
example: |
  "They released the feature with three new safety principles, one blog post, and no mechanism by which those principles could delay launch. It was safety washing with excellent typography."
