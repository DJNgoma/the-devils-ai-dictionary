plainDefinition: |
  A resource link is a reference to external or application-owned context that can be fetched, displayed, or subscribed to by an AI client instead of being copied wholesale into the model prompt.
whyExists: |
  Modern AI clients need access to documents, files, records, datasets, and application state. Links let systems keep context addressable and controlled, while fetching only what the current task actually needs.
misuse: |
  It gets treated as access. A link is not permission, freshness, provenance, or relevance. It is just a route to something that still needs governance.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means clients can show or retrieve context through stable identifiers, while servers keep ownership of the underlying resource and its access rules.
example: |
  "The client passed a resource link to the contract rather than stuffing thirty pages into the context window and hoping attention would become compliance."
