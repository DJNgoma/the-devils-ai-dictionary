plainDefinition: |
  An AI credit pool is an allocated balance of included AI usage credits assigned to an organisation, team, or cost centre. Consumption draws from the pool until its limit is reached, after which use may stop or continue as metered spending.
whyExists: |
  Enterprise AI plans often bundle allowances with licences while usage varies sharply between teams. Pooling credits provides an accounting boundary for distributing included capacity and deciding who may create overages.
misuse: |
  Credits are mistaken for money, compute units, or a hard budget. Their conversion may be vendor-defined, and a pool can coexist with a separate spend limit because included usage and billed overage are different controls.
practicalMeaning: |
  GitHub's Copilot implementation calculates a cost centre's pool from assigned licences and lets administrators block usage at the limit or permit additional spend. Operators still need usage reporting, allocation rules, overage policy, and a clear definition of what one credit buys.
