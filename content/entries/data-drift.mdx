  The quietly important fact that the world keeps changing even after the model card has been written in a firm and reassuring tone.
plainDefinition: |
  Data drift is the change in the statistical or practical character of input data over time, such that the data seen in production no longer matches the data a model, evaluation set, or workflow was designed around.
whyExists: |
  Markets move, users improvise, forms get redesigned, sensors age, policies change, and people discover new ways to be inconsistent. The term exists because models inherit those changes whether the training pipeline feels sentimental about them or not.
misuse: |
  It gets blamed whenever performance falls. Sometimes the inputs truly shifted. Sometimes the labels were weak, the prompt is brittle, the retrieval layer is dirty, or the product team simply changed the job and forgot to tell the model.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means monitoring input distributions, segmenting by cohort and source, checking for broken fields and changed formats, and deciding when retraining, prompt changes, or simple data cleaning will solve more than another round of theoretical concern.
