devilDefinition: |
  A model celebrated for efficiency, usually by people who have just discovered that not every task requires a digital cathedral.
plainDefinition: |
  A small language model is a comparatively lightweight language model designed to deliver acceptable performance with lower memory, compute, latency, or deployment demands than a frontier-scale model.
whyExists: |
  It exists because many business tasks are narrow, repetitive, and unforgiving about latency or cost. A smaller model can be the better engineering choice when the problem is constrained and the environment matters more than prestige.
misuse: |
  It is oversold in two opposite ways: as proof that frontier models are unnecessary for everything, or as if a compact model can casually replace them on hard reasoning and open-ended generation. Both claims are mostly branding with a benchmark attached.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means cheaper inference, easier on-device or edge deployment, tighter control over predictable tasks, and a shorter list of things the system should be trusted to improvise.
example: |
  "The team finally adopted a small language model after spending six months paying frontier-model prices to classify support emails."
