devilDefinition: |
  A company name frequently used as shorthand for an app, a model family, an API, a policy debate, and sometimes the entire industry when people cannot be bothered to separate nouns.
plainDefinition: |
  OpenAI is a company. It builds and sells AI products and services, including model APIs and end-user products such as ChatGPT. The company name is distinct from the names of its individual models and products.
whyExists: |
  The term matters because company, product, and model are constantly collapsed into one another. If you do not separate them, conversations about performance, pricing, safety, procurement, and market position become foggy very quickly.
misuse: |
  People say "OpenAI said this" when they mean ChatGPT produced a reply, or "we use OpenAI" when they actually use a specific API model through a specific product flow. The confusion sounds minor until accuracy, vendor risk, or budgeting starts mattering.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means a commercial vendor relationship. You are choosing a supplier, a set of models, platform terms, product constraints, and an evolving roadmap. The most important question is usually not whether OpenAI exists, but which exact OpenAI product or model you mean.
example: |
  "The board memo said the company was 'using OpenAI', which turned out to mean one customer-service workflow calling a single model through the API with very strict prompt scaffolding."
