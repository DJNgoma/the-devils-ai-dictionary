  Buying intelligence wholesale from one provider, retailing it through a cleaner interface, and hoping the margin survives long enough to become a strategy.
plainDefinition: |
  Inference arbitrage is the practice of profiting from differences in model price, latency, quality, or access by routing demand across providers and packaging the result as a product or service.
whyExists: |
  Model markets are uneven. Providers price differently, regions behave differently, and many users would rather pay one intermediary than personally compare latency tables like a day trader with a prompt addiction.
misuse: |
  The term is stretched when any wrapper, gateway, or comparison site is described as though routing alone were a durable moat. Arbitrage can be real. It is also famously allergic to permanence.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means mixing models behind the scenes, swapping suppliers when pricing shifts, and capturing some spread through UX, bundling, reliability, or customer ignorance. The better businesses add real workflow value. The thinner ones are mostly tariffs with branding.
