  A planning budget is the time, tokens, steps, or cost an agent may spend decomposing a task, exploring options, and deciding a course of action before execution.
whyExists: |
  Agents need planning to avoid reckless action, but unlimited planning becomes latency, expense, and sometimes a decorative loop. Budgets keep exploration proportional to the task.
misuse: |
  It gets confused with caution. A long plan can still be shallow, and a short plan can be appropriate when the task is routine and reversible.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means setting step limits, deciding when to ask a user, when to call tools, when to stop researching, and when to execute a good-enough plan.
