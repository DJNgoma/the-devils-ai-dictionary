plainDefinition: |
  Paper is a design platform built around a web-native canvas, where teams can design, share, and ship interface work with code, data, and agent workflows closer to the same surface.
whyExists: |
  It exists because visual design, prototype review, implementation, and AI-assisted iteration often happen in separate tools that translate badly into one another. A web-standard canvas tries to make the artefact more portable.
misuse: |
  It can be oversold as if an HTML/CSS-aware canvas eliminates product design judgement, engineering taste, or the awkward final mile where a pretty surface has to survive real application state.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means a design space aimed at reducing handoff loss: designers, agents, code, and web output operate closer together, so a prototype has a better chance of becoming implementation material rather than a decorative screenshot.
