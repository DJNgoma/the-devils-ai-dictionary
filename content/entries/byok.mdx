devilDefinition: |
  The generous feature by which a startup lets you pay someone else directly for the expensive part.
plainDefinition: |
  BYOK means "Bring Your Own Key". In AI products it usually means the customer supplies their own API credentials for the underlying model or service provider while the product supplies the interface, workflow, or orchestration.
whyExists: |
  Because model access is costly, margins are fragile, and some customers want tighter control over billing, provider choice, and data handling than a bundled plan allows.
misuse: |
  By presenting it as proof of openness, affordability, or enterprise maturity on its own. Sometimes it is a sensible customer control. Sometimes it is a polite way of admitting the product would rather not carry the inference bill.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means the customer pays the model provider directly, inherits that provider's rate limits and policies, and still pays the application for the workflow wrapped around the key.
example: |
  "The app was cheap right up until we noticed the pricing page quietly assumed BYOK and therefore our own model invoice."
