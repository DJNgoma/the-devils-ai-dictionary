  The place where intelligence becomes a zoning meeting with cooling towers.
plainDefinition: |
  An AI data centre is a facility designed or adapted for AI workloads, especially dense GPU or accelerator clusters that need high power delivery, cooling, networking, storage, physical security, and operational reliability.
whyExists: |
  It exists because large-scale training and high-volume inference are not abstract cloud vapour. They require land, substations, chips, water or advanced cooling, fibre, financing, technicians, and a local community asked to accept the footprint.
misuse: |
  It is misused when any data centre with fashionable tenants becomes an AI data centre, or when megawatts are announced as if they were model capability. Power, racks, and press releases are ingredients, not intelligence.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means site selection, grid interconnection, power purchase agreements, cooling design, cluster networking, accelerator supply, resilience planning, noise and water scrutiny, permits, financing, and a realistic answer for who gets the capacity.
example: |
  "The AI data centre promised national capability, pending the minor administrative details of electricity, chips, permits, and neighbours."
