  A short-lived permission object for one action—security's way of saying 'not a forever API key taped under the keyboard.'
plainDefinition: |
  A capability token is a credential that grants a specific, usually narrow and time-limited authority to perform an action or access a resource, often used for agents and tools.
whyExists: |
  Broad long-lived secrets are a disaster in agentic systems. Capability tokens encode least privilege more naturally.
misuse: |
  Issued with wide scopes and long TTLs, or logged in plaintext traces. Then they are just fancy passwords.
practicalMeaning: |
  Mint per-task tokens, bind audience and path, expire aggressively, and never place raw tokens in prompts if avoidable.
