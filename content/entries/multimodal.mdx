devilDefinition: |
  Able to juggle text, images, audio, or video, which sounds far more coherent than the reality of several data types sharing one product team.
plainDefinition: |
  Multimodal refers to models or systems that can process, generate, or jointly reason over more than one data modality, such as text, image, audio, or video.
whyExists: |
  Human tasks rarely arrive as clean text alone. Multimodal systems exist because many useful applications involve documents, screenshots, spoken language, visual context, or combinations of these.
misuse: |
  It gets waved around as a prestige adjective even when a product merely accepts an image upload and produces a caption. True multimodal capability is not just accepting multiple inputs. It is being able to use them meaningfully for the task at hand.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means a model or stack that can ingest several types of data, map them into compatible internal representations, and produce outputs that reflect those combined signals. The hard part is often not modality support itself but maintaining reliability across them.
example: |
  "The feature was multimodal in the useful sense: it could read the chart, listen to the spoken explanation, and answer questions about the mismatch."
