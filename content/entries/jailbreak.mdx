  The art of persuading a safety system that the rules were meant for someone else.
plainDefinition: |
  A jailbreak is a prompt or interaction pattern intended to bypass a model's safety rules, system instructions, or refusal policies and elicit behaviour it is supposed to restrict.
whyExists: |
  It exists because instruction-following systems are probabilistic, layered defences are imperfect, and many users are curious, mischievous, adversarial, or simply unwilling to accept "no" from a machine.
misuse: |
  The term gets stretched to cover any successful prompt tweak. More precisely, a jailbreak is about overriding the intended boundary, not merely asking the question more clearly.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means red-team exercises, defence updates, moderation tuning, prompt hardening, and a permanent reminder that a polite interface does not imply a secure one, especially once tools and external systems are attached.
