  The slow failure mode where the model has not become stupider; the filing system has merely started lying more politely.
plainDefinition: |
  Retrieval drift is the gradual degradation of a retrieval system's ability to surface current, relevant, and authorised material as documents, embeddings, metadata, permissions, or user queries change over time.
whyExists: |
  Retrieval systems age. Corpora change, language changes, ranking assumptions stop fitting the traffic, and document hygiene rarely improves under pressure. The term exists so teams can name that decay instead of blaming every grounded failure on the model.
misuse: |
  It gets confused with generic data drift or used as a vague excuse for any bad answer. Retrieval drift is specifically about the relevance and freshness of what gets found and passed forward, not about every possible quality problem in the stack.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means stale indexes, outdated embeddings, renamed concepts, broken metadata filters, permission mismatches, and rerankers still optimised for the world as it looked three quarters ago.
