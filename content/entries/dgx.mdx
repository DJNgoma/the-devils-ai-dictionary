  Because many teams do not want to assemble their own AI compute stack from scratch, and NVIDIA would understandably prefer to sell the whole temple rather than only the candles.

  In practice it usually means dense, vendor-shaped compute for organisations willing to pay for an integrated system, faster deployment, and closer alignment with NVIDIA's view of how serious AI infrastructure should look.

DGX is what happens when the GPU story becomes an appliance story. It is not just more chips. It is a deliberate attempt to make the surrounding system part of the sale.
