plainDefinition: |
  Data poisoning is an attack that inserts or manipulates training, fine-tuning, or retrieval data to induce targeted misbehaviour, backdoors, or degraded performance.
whyExists: |
  Models inherit their data. Adversaries noticed that influencing the corpus can influence the model more cheaply than breaking production later.

  Used loosely for any bad data quality. Poisoning implies adversarial intent or strategically planted artefacts, not only messiness.
practicalMeaning: |
  Harden data supply chains, review fine-tune corpora, monitor retrieval indexes for suspicious inserts, and test for trigger behaviours.
