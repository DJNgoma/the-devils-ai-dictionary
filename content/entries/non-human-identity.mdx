  Non-human identity is an identity used by a machine, service, script, workload, bot, or agent rather than a human user.

  Modern systems are full of actors that need credentials but do not have a body, a payslip, or an HR exit interview. AI agents make the category more visible because they combine machine credentials with goal-directed behaviour and tool use.

  It is misused when teams treat all non-human identities as boring plumbing. A deployment key, a data pipeline, and an agent that can negotiate refunds may all be non-human, but they do not carry the same behavioural risk.

  In practice it means inventorying service accounts, workload identities, agent principals, secrets, certificates, tokens, owners, permissions, rotation schedules, and the awkward number of credentials no one remembers creating.
