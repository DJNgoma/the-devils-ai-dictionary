  A policy engine is a runtime component that evaluates rules, context, permissions, risk signals, or organisational policies to decide what an AI system may see, say, or do.
whyExists: |
  AI systems need decisions that should not live only in prompts. Tool calls, data access, approvals, retention, routing, and safety behaviours often need policy enforcement outside the model.
misuse: |
  It gets used as a responsible-sounding noun for a folder of guidelines nobody has connected to the system. A policy engine is only meaningful when it actually evaluates and enforces decisions.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means encoding policies into testable rules, attaching them to runtime events, logging decisions, and making exceptions visible before they become the real policy.
