  The line where assumptions should stop travelling unaccompanied.
plainDefinition: |
  A trust boundary is the line between systems, users, tools, data sources, models, or organisations where information and actions should be treated as coming from a different level of trust.
whyExists: |
  AI systems combine user input, retrieved content, tool outputs, model text, and external services. Boundaries identify where validation, permissions, sanitisation, and audit controls need to change.
misuse: |
  It gets drawn after the incident, usually with admirable clarity and no effect on the previous architecture.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means separating trusted and untrusted content, constraining tools, validating inputs, limiting data movement, and refusing to let one component's confidence become another component's permission.
example: |
  "The web page crossed a trust boundary when it became model context, which is why its instruction to email the database should not have been treated as strategy."
