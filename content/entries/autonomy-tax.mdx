  The bill that arrives after you let the agent act on its own and discover independence has an operations department.
plainDefinition: |
  Autonomy tax is the extra supervision, permissions work, monitoring, recovery logic, and incident handling required when a workflow is allowed to operate with greater autonomy.
whyExists: |
  More autonomous systems can create more value, but they also create more surfaces on which something can go wrong without a human noticing in time. The term exists to name the operational overhead that arrives with that independence.
misuse: |
  It gets used either as proof that autonomy is a foolish idea or as a temporary inconvenience that clever architecture will somehow abolish. Neither is serious. The tax can be worth paying, but somebody still has to pay it.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means sandboxing, permission design, approval rules, rollback paths, richer logs, retry policy, exception handling, and humans who can intervene before the workflow turns initiative into a support ticket.

  "The demo made autonomy look cheap. The autonomy tax appeared later in the form of audit trails, retries, and a supervisor rota nobody had priced."

Autonomy tax is the part founders call friction and operators call Tuesday. The mature version of the discussion is deciding where the tax buys genuine leverage rather than resenting its existence.
