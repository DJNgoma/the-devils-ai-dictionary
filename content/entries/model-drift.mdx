devilDefinition: |
  The moment a system that used to behave one way quietly acquires new manners, new vices, or a fresh interpretation of your instructions, and no dashboard feels personally responsible.
plainDefinition: |
  Model drift is the change in a model system's behaviour over time, whether because the underlying model changed, surrounding prompts and tools changed, traffic changed, or the environment stopped being the one the original evaluation had in mind.
whyExists: |
  AI systems are not static artefacts. Providers update models, teams revise prompts, tools change their schemas, and user behaviour wanders off. The term exists because quality can move even when nobody thinks they touched the main lever.
misuse: |
  It gets used as a polite synonym for "something got worse". Sometimes the issue is genuine behaviour drift. Sometimes it is bad retrieval, a broken tool, a prompt edit, or a team discovering that last month's benchmark did not survive contact with this month's traffic.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means running comparisons over time, holding out stable evaluation sets, watching sensitive slices rather than averages, and keeping enough version history to identify whether the model changed or the system around it did.
example: |
  "The team called it model drift because that sounded dignified. The incident report later revealed a prompt change, a tool timeout, and optimism."
