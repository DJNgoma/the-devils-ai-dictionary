  Asking the system to improvise on first contact and congratulating yourself when probability looks like generality.
plainDefinition: |
  Zero-shot refers to asking a model to perform a task without giving task-specific examples in the prompt.
whyExists: |
  It is useful to distinguish between tasks a model can handle from instructions alone and tasks that need demonstrations, schemas, retrieval, or tighter scaffolding.
misuse: |
  By treating zero-shot success as proof of broad understanding rather than a description of prompt setup. Many so-called zero-shot wins rely on hidden context, careful wording, or tasks that were never especially demanding.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means the cheapest first attempt. If the task is routine and the instructions are clear, zero-shot can be enough. If the task is messy, high-stakes, or structurally ambiguous, it often becomes a polite way to under-specify the work.

Zero-shot is attractive because it feels like generality. It is also attractive because it postpones the adult work of specifying the task properly.
