devilDefinition: |
  A common language for agents, merchants, and payment providers, because commerce had not yet produced enough integration work.
plainDefinition: |
  Universal Commerce Protocol is an open commerce standard for communication among agent platforms, businesses, payment service providers, and credential providers. It defines discoverable capabilities for checkout, identity linking, orders, and payment-token exchange across transports including REST, MCP, and A2A.
whyExists: |
  Agentic shopping otherwise requires one-off integrations for every merchant, platform, and payment provider. UCP supplies shared schemas and composable capabilities so an agent can discover what a business supports and conduct a transaction through a consistent interface.
misuse: |
  The word "universal" invites people to describe an ecosystem ambition as present interoperability. A specification can reduce integration variance; it cannot compel merchants, wallets, platforms, or national payment rules to agree on the rest.
practicalMeaning: |
  A merchant publishes a profile of supported capabilities and extensions. An agent or platform can then negotiate checkout, identity, order, and payment flows without learning a bespoke API for each shop. Adoption and conformance determine whether the promise reaches the till.
