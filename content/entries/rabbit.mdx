  Rabbit is an AI hardware and software company best known for the rabbit r1, a small device built around voice interaction, app actions, and an operating layer called rabbitOS. Its original pitch was that AI should not just answer questions, but also help perform actions across services and workflows; its current positioning also leans into rabbitOS 2, DLAM, and third-party integrations such as OpenClaw on r1.

Rabbit is useful partly because it makes an old ambition newly legible. People do not merely want smarter chat. They want fewer menus, fewer tabs, and fewer tiny acts of digital housekeeping.

The risk, as ever, is that "fewer apps" becomes "one more device".
