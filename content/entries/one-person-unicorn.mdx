devilDefinition: |
  The recurring fantasy that one founder, several agents, and a cloud invoice can replace an organisation and still count as a company instead of an episode.
plainDefinition: |
  The "one-person unicorn" is the idea that AI tools and automation could let a single founder build and run a company worth a billion dollars with little or no traditional staff.
whyExists: |
  It exists because AI has genuinely reduced the cost of coding, design, support, research, and back-office work enough for solo founders to reach farther than they could a few years ago. That makes the old dream of extreme leverage newly discussable and newly distortive.
misuse: |
  The term gets used as if headcount were simply a regrettable inefficiency. It understates the boring things companies are still made of: trust, sales, operations, compliance, customer support, and the general inconvenience of reality needing more than one pair of eyes.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means smaller teams doing more, solo founders getting to revenue faster, and investors testing how much organisational fragility they can rename as leverage.
