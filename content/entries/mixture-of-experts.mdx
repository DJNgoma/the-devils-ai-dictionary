  A mixture-of-experts model is a neural architecture in which a routing mechanism activates only a subset of specialised sub-networks for each token or input, allowing higher total capacity without using all parameters on every pass.
whyExists: |
  Because model builders want more capacity and performance without paying the full compute cost of dense models at every step. Sparse activation offers a way to scale capability more economically, at least in theory.

  By treating "experts" as if the model contains little professors with stable domains of knowledge. It is an optimisation strategy, not a panel discussion. The routing can help a great deal, but it does not grant wisdom by committee.

  In practice it usually means better parameter-efficiency on paper, more complex training and serving behaviour, routing overhead, and a fresh set of engineering questions about load balancing, latency spikes, and uneven quality.

The name flatters the machinery. What matters is not that there are experts, but whether the router sends work to the right places often enough to justify the complications.
