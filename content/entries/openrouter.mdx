  A toll road for developers who want access to every model and one more month before choosing a real dependency.
plainDefinition: |
  OpenRouter is a gateway service that exposes many AI models behind a unified API, letting developers compare providers, route traffic, and manage access through one integration layer.
whyExists: |
  Because the model market is fragmented, provider APIs differ, and teams prefer one contract when they are still shopping for capability, price, latency, or fallback options.
misuse: |
  By implying that abstraction removes vendor risk rather than merely relocating it. A router can hide some differences. It cannot abolish model quirks, pricing shocks, policy changes, or provider outages.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means a gateway for multi-model experimentation, centralised billing, fallback logic, and the comforting illusion that indecision has become architecture.
