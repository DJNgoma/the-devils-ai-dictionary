plainDefinition: |
  A vision-language-action model combines visual observations and language instructions to predict actions for a robot or embodied system. Its output may be low-level controls, action tokens, or higher-level plans that another controller executes.
whyExists: |
  Robots need to connect what they see and what people ask with what their bodies can safely do. VLA models aim to reuse broad visual and linguistic representations while learning action policies across tasks and environments.
misuse: |
  The label is stretched into "general-purpose robot intelligence" after a model succeeds in a curated demonstration. Recognising objects, following familiar instructions, and controlling hardware reliably under physical variation remain different achievements.
practicalMeaning: |
  Evaluate the robot, sensors, action space, training demonstrations, task distribution, recovery behaviour, latency, and safety controller together. A capable model can still fail because the camera moved, the gripper slipped, or the room contained an object the benchmark politely omitted.
