plainDefinition: |
  A context perimeter is the boundary around the information that may enter a prompt, retrieval result, memory store, tool output, or agent workspace for a particular task.
whyExists: |
  AI systems assemble answers from many sources: user prompts, documents, memory, tools, policies, and previous turns. The perimeter names the control surface for deciding what belongs inside that assembled working context.
misuse: |
  It is weakened when teams treat context as a bigger bucket rather than a governed boundary. More context can mean more leakage, more poisoning, more stale assumptions, and more authority accidentally handed to the model.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means defining allowed sources, excluding sensitive or irrelevant material, filtering retrieved chunks, separating tenants and roles, pruning stale memory, and logging what crossed the boundary when an answer mattered.
example: |
  "The support agent needed a tighter context perimeter, not another million tokens of customer history and hope."
