devilDefinition: |
  A confidence shop, stocked with benchmark packs, review panels, and certificates of seriousness for teams whose own edge cases remain inconveniently proprietary.
plainDefinition: |
  An eval marketplace is a vendor or platform that sells evaluation datasets, benchmark tasks, scoring rubrics, human review, automated checks, or testing services for AI models and AI systems.
whyExists: |
  It exists because most teams need broader evaluation coverage than they can build alone, and because shared tests can make vendor claims easier to compare. Buying evaluation capacity can be sensible when it complements internal evidence.
misuse: |
  It is misused when a purchased test is treated as proof that the actual product is safe, useful, or production-ready. A marketplace eval may measure a general capability, a tidy benchmark, or someone else's risk appetite, not the failure modes waiting in your workflow.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means procurement, test selection, data licensing, rater quality, leakage checks, benchmark relevance, custom scenario design, and the dull but necessary work of mapping bought evidence to local release gates.
example: |
  "They bought an eval marketplace subscription, then learned that none of the tests involved their refund policy, their users, or their oddly persuasive chatbot."
