  The fear that if the machines keep learning from one another's homework, the class average will eventually become the curriculum.
plainDefinition: |
  Model collapse is the hypothesised degradation that can occur when models are trained repeatedly on synthetic or model-generated data without enough fresh, high-quality human data to preserve diversity and fidelity.
whyExists: |
  Because synthetic data is useful, cheap, and increasingly tempting at scale, but feedback loops can distort distributions, wash out edge cases, and quietly teach systems to imitate their own errors.
misuse: |
  By treating it as a guaranteed apocalypse or as proof that synthetic data is worthless. Synthetic data can be valuable. The concern is about unmanaged feedback loops, weak provenance, and replacing judgement with volume.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it usually means teams need stronger data lineage, better curation, and honest evaluation before they let generated material dominate the training mix just because it is abundant and conveniently machine-readable.

The phrase has become a general anxiety container, which is understandable. Still, the useful question is not whether collapse is coming in the abstract, but whether your data pipeline has the manners to notice contamination before it institutionalises it.
