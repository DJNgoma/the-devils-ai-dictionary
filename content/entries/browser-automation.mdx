  The respectable practice of teaching software to click through websites until one minor DOM change turns confidence into archaeology.
plainDefinition: |
  Browser automation is the use of scripts or AI systems to control a web browser in order to navigate pages, fill forms, extract information, run tests, or complete repetitive web-based workflows.
whyExists: |
  It exists because large portions of modern work still happen inside web interfaces rather than clean APIs. Teams need ways to test products, collect data, and operate third-party systems without hiring someone to spend the week clicking "next".
misuse: |
  It gets oversold as if navigating one polished demo path proves durable operational intelligence. In reality, cookie banners, CAPTCHAs, flaky selectors, login policies, and small UI changes remain perfectly capable of ending the revolution before lunch.
practicalMeaning: |
  In practice it means selectors, session handling, retries, screenshots, rate limits, anti-bot defences, and constant suspicion that the page will load something slightly different in production. If AI is involved, add another layer of interpretation and another category of excuses.

Browser automation is useful when the work genuinely lives in the browser. It becomes a self-own when teams use it to avoid admitting that the integration surface was broken all along.
