The Devil's AI Dictionary is a pocket reference for the language of artificial intelligence — the real terms, the marketing terms, and the ones that try to be both.

Each entry gives you two things: a plain-English definition that treats you like an adult, and a devil's definition that says the quiet part out loud. No hedging, no glossy vendor copy, no listicle padding.

What you get:

• An A–Z dictionary you can browse like a book, not a search engine
• Fast local search with filters by category and tag
• Category browsing for the big themes: hype, safety, evaluation, products, data, infrastructure
• A daily "Today's word" rotation so you pick up one solid idea at a time
• Save your reading place and come back to it later
• A random entry button for the idly curious
• Dark mode, offline-first entry content, no accounts, no trackers
• A notification each day when a new word goes up (opt-in)

Who it's for:

People who have to read AI news, sit through AI pitches, buy AI products, or explain AI to someone else — and who would prefer to do it without being quietly lied to. Engineers, PMs, analysts, journalists, founders, and anyone who has ever nodded politely at the word "agentic".

The dictionary is deliberately editorial. It is written, not generated. It has a point of view. It is small on purpose.

If you want a sober, well-written reference that respects your time and your intelligence, this is for you.
