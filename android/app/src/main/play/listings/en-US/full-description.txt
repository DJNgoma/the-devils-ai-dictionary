The Devil's AI Dictionary is a sceptical field guide to the language machines, marketers, founders, and consultants use when they want to sound smarter than they are.

It is not a SaaS dashboard. It is a reference book you can read on your phone — plain-English definitions, a Devil's-dictionary edge, and enough structure to actually use when someone drops "agentic", "RAG", or "foundation model" in a meeting.

Every entry has:
• A Devil's definition — the cynical, one-line truth
• A plain definition — what it actually means
• Why it exists — the incentive behind the jargon
• How it gets misused — the common sleight of hand
• What to ask next — the question that cuts through the pitch

Browse by category, search the whole catalogue, save your place, and read a new "Today's word" each day.

Features
• Full offline dictionary — no account, no network, no tracking
• Daily word with a gentle editorial slant
• Category browsing — models, agents, hype, vendors, safety, and more
• Local search across titles, aliases, and body text
• Four typographic themes including a dark "Night" mode
• Saved reading place so you can pick up where you left off

The app ships with the full catalogue bundled on-device. It works on a plane, on a mountain, and at conferences with hostile Wi-Fi.

Free. No ads. No in-app purchases. No analytics. Open source on GitHub.
